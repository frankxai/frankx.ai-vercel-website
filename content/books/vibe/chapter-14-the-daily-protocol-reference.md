## Daily Protocol (90 minutes)

### Minutes 0-5: State Check

Three numbers. Honest assessment. No overthinking.

| Dimension | Scale | Action if Below 5 |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Energy | 1-10 | Prioritize recovery over production |
| Clarity | 1-10 | Re-read strategy document |
| Motivation | 1-10 | Work anyway — motivation follows action |

Log these numbers in your evidence journal.

### Minutes 5-15: RAS Priming

Read your identity statement aloud. Review today's priority (just one — not three, not five, one). Visualize the completed output for 60 seconds. This primes the Reticular Activating System to filter for relevant opportunities and information throughout the day.

**Your identity statement format:**
"I am a [role] who [primary activity]. I build [what you build] for [who you serve]."

Example: "I am a builder who architects intelligence systems. I build tools and books for creators who want to produce at scale."

### Minutes 15-75: Compound Work

Sixty uninterrupted minutes. One compound activity.

**Before starting:**
- Phone in another room (not on silent — in another room)
- Email closed
- Slack closed
- Browser tabs: only what is needed for the current task
- AI tools loaded with relevant skill file

**Compound activities (choose one):**
- Write a chapter or article
- Build a product feature
- Record a course module
- Compose music
- Design a system component
- Develop a new skill

**Non-compound activities (do not choose):**
- Email
- Social media
- Meetings
- Administrative tasks
- Content consumption

### Minutes 75-85: Distribution

Amplify what you created. AI handles the format transformation.

1. Publish the content to its primary platform
2. Generate 2-3 social media versions
3. Queue for newsletter inclusion
4. Update the content library index

### Minutes 85-90: Evidence Log

Three entries. Three sentences. Five minutes.

```
Date: [today]
Built: [what compound asset did I create?]
Signal: [what evidence of progress did I observe?]
Tomorrow: [what is my one priority for tomorrow?]
```

## Weekly Protocol (30 minutes, Sunday)

### Step 1: Evidence Review (5 min)

Scan the week's evidence log entries. Highlight surprises — signals that were unexpected, positive or negative.

### Step 2: Dashboard Update (5 min)

| Metric | This Week | Direction | Trend (3 weeks) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Compound Output Rate | | ↑ ↓ → | |
| Email Growth Rate | | ↑ ↓ → | |
| Content Engagement Depth | | ↑ ↓ → | |
| Revenue Per Subscriber | | ↑ ↓ → | |
| Sustainability Score | | ↑ ↓ → | |

Three consecutive weeks in the same direction = a trend worth investigating.

### Step 3: Three Questions (10 min)

1. What produced the best result this week?
   → Do more of it next week.

2. What produced the worst result this week?
   → Understand why. Adjust.

3. What will I test next week?
   → One small experiment.

### Step 4: Next Week's Priorities (10 min)

Three priorities. Not twelve. Three.

```
Priority 1: [most important compound activity]
Priority 2: [second most important]
Priority 3: [third most important]
```

If a fourth priority wants to be listed, one of the first three is not actually a priority.

## Implementation Notes

**Start imperfect.** The protocol does not need to be executed perfectly to produce results. A 70% execution rate beats a 0% execution rate. Do not let perfectionism prevent you from starting.

**Adapt to your rhythm.** The 90-minute daily protocol can run at any time — morning, afternoon, evening, middle of the night. Run it when your energy is highest. Not when productivity culture says you should.

**Track for patterns, not for judgment.** The evidence log and dashboard are not report cards. They are sensors. Sensors detect signal. They do not assign grades. Use the data to understand, not to judge.

**Be patient with the compound curve.** Months 1-3: the curve is flat. Months 4-6: the curve begins to bend. Months 7-12: the curve accelerates. The flat period is where most people quit. Do not quit during the flat period.

**Trust the architecture.** The principles — compound effects, RAS priming, environmental design, antifragility, AI amplification — are not opinions. They are mechanisms with documented evidence. Trust the mechanisms. Run the protocols. Collect the evidence.

The evidence will speak for itself.
