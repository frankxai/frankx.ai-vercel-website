**TL;DR** — Your soul.md is a markdown file that defines who you are as a creator across 7 dimensions: Energy, Mind, Craft, Voice, Capital, Circle, and Legacy. You fill it honestly, review it weekly, and update it monthly. It becomes your creative compass. Here is exactly how to build one.

---

## What Is soul.md?

If you use Claude Code, you know about `CLAUDE.md` — the configuration file that tells AI how to work with your project. Your `soul.md` is the same concept, but for your life as a creator.

It is a plain markdown file. It lives in your home directory. It contains your honest self-assessment across 7 dimensions. And it serves one purpose: **keeping you aligned with who you are becoming, not just who you are today.**

```markdown
# soul.md — Your GenCreator Operating File

## Identity
- Name: Frank Riemer
- Craft: AI Architecture + Music + Content
- Stack: Claude, Suno, n8n, Next.js, Vercel
- Mission: Ship daily. Compound relentlessly.
```

## The 7 Dimensions

### 1. Energy

Your creative output is directly proportional to your energy management. This dimension tracks:

- **Peak hours** — When are you most creative? Protect those hours.
- **Movement practice** — Physical activity is not optional for sustained creation.
- **Recovery ritual** — How do you recharge? What stops burnout?
- **Score (1-10)** — Be honest. If you are burned out, that is a 3.

**Scoring guide:**
- 1-3: Burnout cycles, no routine, reactive to energy levels
- 4-6: Some routine, inconsistent, occasional crashes
- 7-9: Optimized peak hours, regular movement, proactive recovery
- 10: Sustainable machine — you can ship daily without burning out

### 2. Mind

Learning velocity is a GenCreator superpower. This dimension tracks:

- **Currently learning** — What specific skill or domain?
- **Books this month** — Reading compounds. Track it.
- **Mental model I use most** — Compounding? First principles? Leverage?
- **Learning velocity (1-10)** — How fast are you applying what you learn?

The difference between consuming and learning is **application**. If you read about prompt engineering but never write a prompt, your Mind score is low.

### 3. Craft

This is the core — your primary skill and how deeply you have developed it.

- **Primary skill** — One thing. What is your main craft?
- **Secondary skills** — What stacks with your primary?
- **Current project** — What are you shipping right now?
- **Craft score (1-10)** — How deep is your expertise?

GenCreators have a **skill stack**: complementary skills that amplify each other. AI architecture + music production + content writing is a stack. Each skill makes the others more valuable.

### 4. Voice

The hardest dimension for most creators. Your voice is your unique perspective — the thing that makes your work distinctly yours.

- **My perspective on** — What topic do you have a strong opinion about?
- **What I would say if no one judged** — Your most honest creative statement.
- **Writing style in 3 words** — How does your voice sound?
- **Voice clarity (1-10)** — Can people recognize your work without seeing your name?

A 10 here means you have a voice so distinct that people can identify your work blind. Most creators start at 3-4.

### 5. Capital

Creativity without revenue is a hobby. Capital tracks your financial sustainability.

- **Revenue streams** — Products, consulting, content, sponsorships?
- **Monthly target** — What do you need to sustain full-time creation?
- **Highest-leverage activity** — What single activity generates the most value?
- **Financial health (1-10)** — Can you create without financial anxiety?

The GenCreator framework emphasizes **leverage** — digital products, automated systems, and compounding assets over trading time for money.

### 6. Circle

No creator succeeds alone. Circle tracks your relationships and community.

- **Mentors** — Who do you learn from? (Can be people you follow, not just personal mentors)
- **Peers** — Who are you building alongside?
- **Community I serve** — Who benefits from your work?
- **Network strength (1-10)** — Do you have people to call when stuck?

### 7. Legacy

The long game. What are you building that outlives this year?

- **What I want to leave behind** — Your creative legacy statement.
- **System that works without me** — Automation, content, products that keep working.
- **10-year vision** — Where is this all going?
- **Legacy clarity (1-10)** — Can you articulate your endgame?

## How to Use It

### Weekly Review (15 minutes)

Every week, open your soul.md and ask:

1. Did my actions this week align with my Identity section?
2. Which dimension got the most attention? Which got neglected?
3. What one thing would improve my lowest-scoring dimension?

### Monthly Update (30 minutes)

Once a month, re-score every dimension. Update the fields. Notice the trends.

- Are you growing in Craft but declining in Energy?
- Is your Voice getting clearer or more diluted?
- Is Capital moving in the right direction?

### Quarterly Deep Review (1 hour)

Every quarter, rewrite your Identity section from scratch. Your mission, stack, and craft may have evolved. Let the file reflect reality.

## FAQ

### Where do I store my soul.md?

Anywhere you will see it. `~/soul.md` is the convention. Some creators keep it in their project repo, others in Obsidian or Notion.

### How honest should I be?

Completely. This file is for you. A 3 in Voice is not failure — it is awareness. You can not improve what you do not measure.

### Can I share my soul.md?

Up to you. Some GenCreators share their soul.md publicly as an accountability tool. Others keep it private. Both approaches work.

### How often should scores change?

Monthly changes of 1-2 points are normal. If nothing changes in 3 months, something is stuck.

### What if I score low on everything?

Then you have a clear roadmap. Pick the one dimension that would unlock the most progress, and focus there for 30 days. Your soul.md just gave you your next move.
