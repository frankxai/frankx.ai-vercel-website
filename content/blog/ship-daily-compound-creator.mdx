## The Math of Daily Shipping

Most creators ship once a week. Some ship once a month. GenCreators ship daily.

Here is what that looks like over time:

| Timeframe | Weekly shipper | Daily shipper |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| 1 month | 4 artifacts | 30 artifacts |
| 3 months | 12 artifacts | 90 artifacts |
| 6 months | 24 artifacts | 180 artifacts |
| 1 year | 52 artifacts | 365 artifacts |

But it gets better. Daily shipping compounds. Each artifact links to previous work, builds on previous skills, and creates more surface area for discovery.

## What Counts as "Shipping"?

A ship is anything you put into the world that did not exist yesterday:

- A blog post (even 300 words)
- A song (even a rough draft on Suno)
- A product update or feature
- A community post with real insight
- A newsletter edition
- An open-source contribution
- A social post with original thought

The key distinction: **a ship changes the world outside your head.** It exists where others can see it, use it, respond to it.

## The 3-Tier Shipping System

Every day carries different energy. The system honors that:

### Tier 1: Full Ship (high energy)
- Complete blog post or guide
- Finished song or product feature
- New tool or automation workflow
- Time: 2-4 hours

### Tier 2: Quick Ship (medium energy)
- Short social post with original insight
- Code commit that fixes or improves something
- Community response that helps someone
- Time: 30-60 minutes

### Tier 3: Micro Ship (low energy)
- Journal entry in your soul.md
- One improvement to existing work
- Reading + highlight with personal note
- Time: 15-30 minutes

The rule: **never go to sleep without at least a Tier 3 ship.** This maintains the chain without burning you out.

## Common Objections

### "I do not have time to ship daily"

A Tier 3 ship takes 15 minutes. You have 15 minutes. The real question is priority, not time.

### "My work is not good enough to ship daily"

Shipping is how you get good. Waiting until you are "ready" means never shipping. The first 30 ships will be rough. The next 60 will surprise you.

### "Quality over quantity"

False dichotomy. GenCreators ship high quality at high volume because they use AI as a force multiplier. The constraint is taste and judgment, not bandwidth.

### "What if I run out of ideas?"

You will not. Daily shipping creates a feedback loop: each artifact sparks ideas for the next. The more you ship, the more ideas you have.

## Your Daily Shipping Ritual

1. **Morning (5 min)**: Check your soul.md. What dimension needs attention today?
2. **Peak hours (1-2 hrs)**: Execute your main ship. Use AI to accelerate.
3. **Afternoon (15 min)**: Distribute. Cross-post. Engage with feedback.
4. **Evening (5 min)**: Log the ship. Update your tracker. Plan tomorrow.

Total time: 2-3 hours of focused work. Everything else is your day.

## FAQ

### What tool do you use to track daily shipping?

A simple markdown file or Notion database. Track date, artifact type, and a one-line description. The act of logging reinforces the habit.

### How do you maintain quality at daily pace?

AI handles the production bandwidth. Your job is curation and taste. Not everything ships at the same quality level — Tier 1 ships are polished, Tier 3 ships are functional. Both count.

### What is the best time to ship?

During your peak energy hours (track this in your soul.md). For many, this is early morning. For some, late night. The framework adapts to you.

### How long before compounding kicks in?

Typically 60-90 days. The first month builds habit. The second month builds quality. The third month builds compound returns.

### Can a team use this system?

Yes. Each team member maintains their own shipping cadence. The team ships collectively by aggregating individual outputs. Weekly team reviews replace individual soul.md reviews.
