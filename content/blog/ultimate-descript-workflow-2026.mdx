## What is Descript and how does it work?

Descript is an all-in-one audio and video editor built around transcription. When you import a recording or capture one inside the app, Descript transcribes it automatically across 25 languages. From that point you edit the transcript, not a waveform. Delete a paragraph of text and the corresponding video is gone. Rearrange sentences and the footage reorders to match.

That core is wrapped in a stack of AI tools that handle the tedious work:

- **Voice cloning (Overdub / Regenerate)** — train a model on your voice, then fix a flubbed line by typing the correction. The 2026 Regenerate feature analyzes surrounding tone and room ambience so the patched word blends into the original recording. All synthetic audio carries an invisible watermark.
- **Studio Sound** — regenerative AI that strips background noise, echo, and hiss, then rebuilds voice clarity so a phone recording sounds studio-grade.
- **Remove Filler Words** — automatic detection of "um," "uh," "like," and "you know," removable in one click or selectively.
- **AI Eye Contact** — subtly redirects your gaze toward the lens when you were reading off-screen notes.
- **Underlord** — the 2026 AI assistant that runs the boring passes for you: filler removal, eye contact, and auto-generating social clips from a long video.
- **Screen recorder** — capture screen, camera, and audio inside the app, so tutorials and demos land directly in an editable project.
- **Multi-track editing** — sync multiple speakers or camera angles on separate tracks, each with its own transcript lane.

The point of the setup is not to learn every feature. It is to make the words the interface and let AI handle the cleanup.

## What is the best podcast workflow in Descript?

This is the workflow Descript was built for. Edit-by-transcript turns a two-hour conversation into a tight episode without a single timeline scrub.

1. **Record or import** all speakers as separate tracks. Multi-track keeps each voice on its own transcript lane, so a cut to one speaker doesn't disturb the others.
2. **Run Studio Sound** across every track. Remote guests on bad mics come out clean.
3. **Remove filler words** in one pass. On a long conversation this alone removes hundreds of "ums" and tightens pacing instantly.
4. **Edit by reading.** Skim the transcript, select the dead air and tangents, delete. Watch your runtime drop in the corner.
5. **Patch mistakes with voice cloning.** Fixed a mispronounced name? Type the correction and let Regenerate blend it in — no callback to the guest.
6. **Export** to your podcast preset, then let Underlord generate show notes and timestamps from the transcript.

A first-pass edit that took an afternoon in Audition becomes a focused hour.

## How do you remove ums and filler words in one click?

Open the project, run **Remove Filler Words** from the editing menu. Descript highlights every detected "um," "uh," "like," and "you know" across the transcript. You get two choices: remove all instances at once, or step through and keep the ones that serve the rhythm. On a Creator plan or above this runs at full strength with no cap on a normal episode.

The discipline here is restraint. Removing every single filler can make speech sound robotic. Keep the natural pauses that carry emphasis; cut the ones that signal hesitation. Two minutes of cleanup that would have taken thirty in a waveform editor.
