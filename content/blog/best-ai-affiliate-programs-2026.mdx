## Why does recurring commission beat a flashy one-time rate?

Run one example. A tool costs $30/month. Program A pays 50% one-time. Program B pays 30% recurring.

- **Program A:** $15. Done. One payment.
- **Program B:** $9/month. After 4 months you've passed Program A. After 12 months you've earned $108 — seven times more.

This is why Systeme.io sits at the top despite not being the most exciting AI tool on the list. 60% of a $97/month plan, paid every month the customer stays, with attribution that never expires, is the strongest unit economics in the category. A handful of sticky referrals becomes a base that pays whether or not you publish anything new that month.

The lesson generalizes: **rank programs by the area under the curve, not the height of the first bar.** A recurring program with a 12-month window (ElevenLabs, HeyGen, Higgsfield, Descript) is a year of compounding. A lifetime program (Systeme.io, Writesonic) is an annuity.

**Is recurring commission really better than a higher one-time rate?**
Almost always, if the product has decent retention. A 30% recurring program passes a 50% one-time program in about four months on a monthly subscription, then keeps compounding. The only time one-time wins is for products people buy once and cancel fast.

**Which programs are closed to new affiliates in 2026?**

**What's the MCP affiliate angle?**
