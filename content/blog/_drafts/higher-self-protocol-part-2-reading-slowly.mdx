## Why we forgot how to read slowly
- Doom-scrolling vs. the 1,500-year-old practice of lectio divina (read, meditate, pray, contemplate).
- The lost skill: staying with one phrase long enough for it to work on you.

## The four movements, with a study partner
- **Lectio (read):** AI for translation, context, the word you didn't understand — not interpretation.
- **Meditatio (chew):** prompts that ask *you* what the phrase stirs, one at a time.
- **Oratio (respond):** kept human and private.
- **Contemplatio (rest):** the AI gets out of the way. Silence is the feature.

## The prompt patterns (copy-paste)
- Pattern 1: "Read this passage with me one verse at a time. For each, give me the literal sense and any key original-language note. Ask nothing. Wait for me to respond before continuing."
- Pattern 2: cross-tradition — same passage, "where does the Dhammapada / Marcus Aurelius point at something similar?"
- Pattern 3: the un-cringe guardrail — "Do not tell me what it means for my life. That's my work."

## Doing it across traditions
- Sermon on the Mount · Dhammapada · Meditations · Tao Te Ching — same method, different mountain.

## The one rule
- The machine supplies context. You supply meaning. The moment that flips, stop.
