## The irony I have to address first
- Using a device built to fragment your attention to *train* your attention. Why it can still work — boundaries, not denial.

## What the traditions actually trained
- Buddhist *sati* (mindfulness) and *samadhi* (concentration).
- Stoic *prosoche* (attention to the present and to your own judgments).
- Same muscle, two vocabularies.

## AI as a mirror, not a meditation teacher
- It does NOT lead the meditation (apps and humans are better for that).
- It DOES help you *notice your own mind*: name the patterns in your reflections, surface the recurring story, ask the Socratic follow-up.
- The "examine your judgments" Stoic loop, run as a dialogue.

## A daily practice
- Sit (no AI). Then reflect (with AI as mirror): "Here's where my mind kept going today. What pattern do you notice? Ask me about it — don't conclude."

## The failure mode
- Replacing the silence with chatter. The point is the sitting. The AI is for *after*.
