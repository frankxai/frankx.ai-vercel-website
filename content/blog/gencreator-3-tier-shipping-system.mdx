**TL;DR** — The 3-Tier Shipping System matches your creative output to your energy level. Tier 1 (Full Ship) is 2-4 hours of deep work. Tier 2 (Quick Ship) is 30-60 minutes. Tier 3 (Micro Ship) is 15-30 minutes. The rule: at least a Tier 3 every day. Consistency compounds. Perfection stalls.

## Why Most Creators Stall

Most creators operate in two modes: marathon sessions or complete silence. They wait for the perfect block of time, the perfect idea, the perfect energy level — and while they wait, nothing gets made.

The GenCreator approach is different. It treats creative output like a muscle that gets stronger with daily use, regardless of the weight you lift on any given day.

## The 3-Tier Framework

### Tier 1: Full Ship (2-4 Hours)

A Full Ship is a substantial piece of work. A complete blog post. A new feature shipped to production. A full music track produced and published. A workshop delivered.

**When to do it:** High energy days. Deep focus available. The ideas are flowing.

**Examples:**

- Publish a 1,500-word technical article
- Ship a new product feature
- Produce and release a complete AI-generated track
- Record and edit a tutorial video
- Build and deploy an automation workflow

### Tier 2: Quick Ship (30-60 Minutes)

A Quick Ship is a focused burst. Something meaningful gets published, but it requires less creative intensity.

**When to do it:** Medium energy. You have a window between meetings or after dinner.

**Examples:**

- Draft a LinkedIn post with original insights
- Update and improve an existing page
- Sketch the outline for next week's article
- Reply thoughtfully in 3 community threads
- Configure a new tool in your stack

### Tier 3: Micro Ship (15-30 Minutes)

A Micro Ship is the minimum viable creation. It keeps the streak alive and the creative muscle engaged.

**When to do it:** Low energy days. Sick days. Travel days. Days where everything is on fire.

**Examples:**

- Write a single tweet with a genuine insight
- Fix one typo, improve one paragraph
- Add one entry to your soul.md
- Comment meaningfully on a peer's work
- Log an idea in your capture system

## The One Rule

**At least a Tier 3 every day.**

Not a Tier 1. Not even a Tier 2. Just something. Something real, something shipped, something that exists in the world that did not exist yesterday.

The streak matters more than the tier. A 90-day streak of Micro Ships builds more creative infrastructure than three months of sporadic marathon sessions.

## How Energy Determines the Tier

GenCreators are energy-aware. They read their state before choosing their tier:

| Energy Signal                    | Tier   | Duration |
| -------------------------------- | ------ | -------- |
| "I could build for hours"        | Tier 1 | 2-4h     |
| "I have a good 45 minutes in me" | Tier 2 | 30-60m   |
| "Today is survival mode"         | Tier 3 | 15-30m   |

This is the opposite of guilt-driven productivity. Low-energy days are expected, planned for, and respected. The system accommodates them instead of pretending they should not exist.

## The Compound Math

Consider two creators over 90 days:

**Creator A** works in bursts: 10 Full Ships, scattered across 3 months, with long gaps between.

**Creator B** uses the 3-Tier System: 15 Full Ships, 30 Quick Ships, 45 Micro Ships. Something every single day.

Creator B has 90 published artifacts. Creator B has a portfolio. Creator B has momentum. Creator B has an audience that shows up because the creator shows up first.

The individual quality of any one Micro Ship is small. But the compound effect of 90 consecutive days of showing up — that is enormous.

## FAQ

### What counts as a "ship"?

Anything real that gets published, posted, or shared externally. A tweet, a commit, a blog post, a comment, a sketch. The key word is _real_ — it exists in the world, visible to at least one other person.

### What if I miss a day?

Start again. The streak resets, but the total ships remain. Missing one day is a data point, not a failure. The practice is in the returning.

### Can I log multiple ships in one day?

Yes. A Tier 1 morning session and a Tier 3 evening micro-ship both count. The tracker records every entry.

### Is this just glorified task tracking?

Task tracking measures completion. Shipping tracking measures creative output that reaches the world. The difference is the _publication_ — something must be visible, real, shipped.

### How does this connect to the GenCreator Framework?

The 3-Tier System is Principle #1 in action: "Ship daily, compound forever." It is the daily practice layer of the framework. The [Handbook](/gencreator/handbook) covers it in Chapter 3.
