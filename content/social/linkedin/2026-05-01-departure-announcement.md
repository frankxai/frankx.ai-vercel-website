The most demanding organizations in the world taught me how AI actually gets built.

End of May, I leave Oracle to teach everyone else.

Seven years ago I joined as a Generation Oracle Trainee. I'm closing the chapter as a GenAI Super Alumni — having helped architect Oracle EMEA's AI Center of Excellence, run workshops for dozens of customers across the continent, and contribute to solution designs and RFPs ranging from smart-city programs to Spain's national metrology authority to the UAE's $100B AI vision.

Along the way I worked GTM with OpenAI, Cohere, Meta, Grok, and Gemini. Onboarded seven AI Architects into the Slack workflows I designed to scale Canvas, content assets, and solution design across the team. Facilitated deals into the eight-figure range with the kind of attention to detail and collaboration that separates real enterprise AI from theater.

I learned what an AI Center of Excellence actually is — not as a slide, but as a living system: Strategy. Governance. Talent. Technology. Data. Ethics.

Then I noticed something I couldn't unsee.

The same six pillars that cost a Fortune 500 millions to stand up — they work for one person. They work for a creator. A family. A school. A founder running three brands from a houseboat.

The cost gap is roughly 5,000×. The outcome gap is closing fast.

So on June 1st I go full-time on the bet I've been quietly compounding on the side: making enterprise-grade AI architecture freely available to individuals — through FrankX, Arcanea, and Starlight Intelligence Systems.

Here's the part I didn't expect to write: I'm walking away from the safest seat at the AI revolution. I have no idea if it works. I have every reason to believe it does.

What I do know: the people I most want to serve aren't in procurement cycles. They're at their kitchen tables at 11pm trying to figure out how to use this stuff to build a life.

That's who I'm building for.

If you've watched me build over the years — thank you. If you're in the middle of the same leap — I see you. If you want to see what an AI CoE looks like for one person, follow along starting June 1.

Oracle gave me the architecture. The next chapter is what I do with it.
