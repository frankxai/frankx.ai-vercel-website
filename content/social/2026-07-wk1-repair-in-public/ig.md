## Slide copy

**S1 — hook (biggest type, one stat)**
> Our AI agents audited our own websites.
> **668 broken assets.**
> The teardown →

**S2 — the twist**
> Nothing was "down."
> Every page returned **200 OK**.
> Monitoring: green.
> That's what makes this failure class dangerous.

**S3 — finding 1**
> **524 images broke from one cause.**
> Cached pages still pointed at deployment URLs that no longer exist.
> Invisible from the editor. Cleared by one fresh deploy.

**S4 — finding 2**
> The newsletter accepted every signup.
> Then silently dropped it.
> The form said "success." The audience never grew.
> Nobody complained — the affected people are the ones you never hear from.

**S5 — finding 3**
> **Zero paid checkouts worked.**
> On any of our sites.
> Product pages collecting clicks that could never convert.

**S6 — the pattern**
> The failures live in the seams:
> cache ↔ deployment
> API ↔ database
> site ↔ payment provider
> Each system "healthy." The connection broken.

**S7 — the method**
> The fix: an agent that browses like a first-time visitor, every day.
> Follows every link. Loads every image.
> Submits every form. Checks every promise.
> Then files a report a human triages.

**S8 — close + CTA**
> We're fixing all of it in public this month.
> 5 fix PRs shipped in week one.
> **Save this** if you ship websites.
> Full report: frankx.ai/research

## Caption

We audited our own production sites with an AI agent swarm — and published what it found.

668 broken assets. A newsletter silently dropping signups. Zero working checkouts. All on sites that looked perfectly healthy from every dashboard we had.

July is open-doors month: the audits, the fixes, and the system behind them, all in public. Full report is on the research hub (link in bio).

If you ship websites with a small team, slide 6 is the one to remember: failures live in the seams between healthy systems.
