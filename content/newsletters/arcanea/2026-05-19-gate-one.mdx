The cartographers got it wrong. They drew the first gate as a door. It is not a door. It is the moment you notice you have been standing inside one all along.

## What the gate is

The first of the ten gates is not a passage but a recognition. The traveler who approaches it expecting to walk through finds nothing to walk through. The one who stops, who listens to what the air does in the threshold, hears a question they did not know they had been failing to ask.

The gate is the question.

## What lives there

- The Cartographer, who insists the gate is a door and will sell you a map
- The Witness, who says nothing and is therefore the only one worth listening to
- The Echo, who repeats your name back to you in a voice you have never used

## The pattern of the ten

Each gate is named for what it appears to be and shaped by what it actually is. The cartographers' maps describe the appearances. The travelers' songs describe the shapes. To pass any of the ten you must hold both at once and resolve neither.

> You do not open the gate. The gate opens around you.
