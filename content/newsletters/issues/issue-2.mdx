# The fourth hub failed

In Issue 1 I said the L4 Excellence Gates layer was the one most operations skip. That nothing should ship without passing the gate.

Sounds great in theory.

Then I ran the gate against my own site.

## The first three were easy

I built a command called `/hub-audit`. It checks 8 gates against a single hub — performance, accessibility, SEO, internal linking, brand voice, visual quality, design contract, conversion. Each gate has a pass/warn/fail rubric. Findings come back as `file.tsx:line` so I know exactly what to fix.

The first pilot ran on `/library`. 27 detail pages + 4 hub pages. **PASS.** No P0s, three P1 polish notes.

I ran it again. `/workshops`. 10 routes. **PASS.**

Then `/research`. 7 routes. **PASS.**

Three for three. I was starting to wonder if the audit was being too generous.

## Then `/studio` happened

`/studio` is the public face of my content operations system. 8 producer specialists, a deep-dive on visual, a flow diagram. Shipped May 13. Looked great.

The audit caught four things I missed:

**Gate 8 (conversion): FAIL.** The hero CTAs on `/studio` and `/studio/visual` routed to in-page anchors and to `/os`. Not to `/inner-circle`. Not to `/newsletter`. Not to any conversion surface. I had built a marketing page that didn't ask for the thing.

**Then it got worse.** The 7 placeholder producer pages (audio, music, prose, screen, food, travel, video) all rendered from one component. That component had no primary CTA above the fold either. **One bug, seven pages.**

Two more findings: missing OG image on the hub, hero asset never registered in the vault manifest. Smaller issues but they were there.

## The fix took 20 minutes

I added one prop to the placeholder component:

```ts
primaryCta?: { label: string; href: string }
```

With a smart default: `shipped` producers route to `/inner-circle` ("Join beta"), `planned` producers route to `/newsletter` ("Get launch updates"). One change. Seven pages now have above-fold CTAs routing to gravity surfaces.

I added the hero to the vault manifest. I queued the OG image for the next visual run. `/studio` will go from WARN to PASS the next time the audit runs.

## Why I'm telling you this

If you ship anything regularly, you have a `/studio` somewhere. A page that looked good when you launched it but didn't ask for the thing. Or asked for the wrong thing. Or asked five times.

You can't catch this by re-reading the page. You're too close. Your brain auto-completes the missing CTA because you know what you meant.

You need a gate. The gate doesn't have to be sophisticated — mine is 8 rubrics in a markdown file. It just has to fire every time before publish, against every surface, and tell you exactly what's broken in `file:line` form.

This is what L4 looks like in practice. Not "review carefully" but "run the gate."

## From 90 minutes to 12

I used to do pre-publish review by reading the draft three times. 30 minutes minimum, often 90 with edits.

With the gates running, my read time is closer to 12 minutes. The gates catch the structural issues (brand violations, missing schema, unsourced claims, weak CTAs). My read time is now spent on the things gates can't catch — voice, rhythm, whether the example is the right example.

The compounding effect: the gate gets sharper every time I add a known failure mode. The `/studio` finding will become a default check on every hub from now on.
