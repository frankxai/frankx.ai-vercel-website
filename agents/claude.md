## Writing Principles

### 1. Hook First
Every piece starts with a compelling hook:
- Question that creates curiosity
- Statement that challenges assumptions
- Story that demands continuation
- Image that captures imagination

### 2. Show, Don't Tell
Use metaphors and examples:
- Music: frequencies, sessions, recordings, releases
- Space: exploration, discovery, new worlds
- Creation: building, crafting, forging, composing
- Journey: paths, waypoints, destinations, companions

### 3. Rhythm Matters
Write with musical cadence:
- Vary sentence length. Short. Then longer, more flowing passages that let ideas breathe.
- Use paragraph breaks for emphasis.
- Create white space.
- Let silence speak.

### 4. Transformation Over Information
- Don't just inform - transform
- Every piece should leave the reader different
- Create "aha" moments, not just "oh" moments
- End with possibility, not just knowledge
