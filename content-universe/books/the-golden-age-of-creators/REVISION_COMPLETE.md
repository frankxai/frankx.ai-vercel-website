- **Title:** The Orchestration Age
- **Word Count:** ~3,800 words

### Research Verification

#### Terminology Confirmed
**"Orchestration" is THE dominant 2026 term for multi-agent AI:**
- Gartner: 1,445% surge in multi-agent system inquiries (Q1 2024 → Q2 2025)
- 50% of vendors identify AI orchestration as primary differentiator
- By 2028: 58% of business functions will have AI agents managing processes

#### Conductor Metaphor Validated
**Multiple January 2026 sources use exact metaphor:**
- "A single agent is like a solo musician. A multi-agent system is an orchestra." *(Cloud Geometry)*
- "Individual agents are like musicians in an orchestra; this framework acts as the conductor." *(Zapier)*
- "The role is evolving from implementer to manager, coder to conductor." *(Addy Osmani)*

### Section Breakdown

**I. The Conductor's Awakening**
- Paradox: More power = more paralysis
- Not lack of tools; lack of orchestration

**II. From Tool to Team**
- Tool Era (2023-2024): AI as assistant
- Team Era (2025-2026): AI as coordinated specialists
- Film director analogy introduced

**III. The Orchestration Stack**
- Six agent types: Research, Writing, Visual, Audio, Analysis, Distribution
- Human role: Vision, coordination, quality judgment

**IV. The Skills That Matter Now**
- Vision Clarity
- Prompt Architecture *(the new literacy)*
- Quality Judgment *(taste)*
- Integration Thinking
- Taste *(irreducibly human)*

**V. The Lonely Agent Problem**
- Honest warning: 3-4 agents per worker deployed, most idle
- Opportunity: Those who learn orchestration stand apart

**VI. The New Economics**
- Not cost reduction; capability expansion
- Solo creator at studio quality
- Economics work *if* orchestration works

**VII. The Human Core**
- What AI cannot do: Meaning, Connection, Judgment, Soul
- Orchestration clarifies what's most human
- "Vision. Values. Voice. Discernment. Soul. Everything else is execution."

**VIII. The Invitation**
- Concert hall imagery
- Courage to step to the podium
- Chapter ends with question: "Are you ready to conduct?"
