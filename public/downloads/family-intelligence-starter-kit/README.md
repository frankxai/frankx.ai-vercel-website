# Family Intelligence Starter Kit

This packet helps a family decide what belongs where before choosing software.

## Use it in this order

1. Adapt `family-circles.yaml` with real people only in your private workspace.
2. Receive family-history statements through `claim-template.yaml`; never promote them directly to facts.
3. Use `consent-and-publication-checklist.de.md` before sharing information about living people.
4. Use `elder-interview.de.md` to record stories with permission and source context.
5. Use `work-order.template.yaml` when handing an approved change from ChatGPT or a family steward to Codex.
6. Use `private-pilot-topology.de.md` to separate household, closest family, wider family, descendants, and godparent relationships.
7. Run `secure-intake-checklist.de.md` before accepting any document or contribution.
8. Use `vercel-and-v0-template.md` to clone the locked runtime or open the privacy-bounded v0 prompt.
9. Adapt `social-launch-template.md` only after a secure intake channel exists.

## Safety boundary

Do not put private family data in a public GitHub repository. Do not collect passports, DNA files, medical records, passwords, precise addresses, or unredacted civil records through ordinary email. Children are never public by default. AI may organize a review packet but cannot determine lineage, consent, incapacity, death, or succession access.

These templates are operational starting points, not legal advice.
