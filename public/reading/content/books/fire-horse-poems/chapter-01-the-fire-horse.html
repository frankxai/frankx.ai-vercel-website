The Fire Horse

The fire horse does not knock. It arrives.

Sixty years between each burning, and in between — the world forgets what it feels like when the ground remembers how to run.

This year is not for the careful. Not for those who measure twice and never cut. Not for the ones who archive their dreams in folders labeled someday.

The fire horse says: today.

Build the thing. Ship the song. Write the chapter. Make the call you've rehearsed in every shower for six months.

The eclipse comes in the morning and a ring of fire hangs in the sky like a question you already know the answer to.

The answer is yes. The answer was always yes. The horse was always coming.

— FrankX, February 2026
