LinkedIn Video Script Template

Video Length: 30 seconds - 3 minutes (90 seconds optimal)
Format: Square (1:1) or Vertical (9:16)
Tone: Professional but personable

LinkedIn Video Structure

HOOK (0-5 seconds)

[VISUAL]: [Professional, attention-grabbing opening]

[TEXT OVERLAY]: [Clear, value-driven statement]

[YOU]: [Compelling opening line]

Hook Types for LinkedIn:

  • Insight: "Here's what [X years] in [industry] taught me..."
  • Contrarian: "Everyone's doing [X] wrong. Here's why..."
  • Question: "Want to know how [successful outcome]?"
  • Statistic: "[X]% of [professionals] don't know this..."
  • Story: "I made a [X] mistake that cost me [Y]..."

CONTENT (5-150 seconds)

Format 1: Professional Insight (60-90 seconds)

Hook (5s): "3 lessons from [X years] in [industry]"

Lesson 1 (25s):
- State the lesson
- Brief example
- Why it matters

Lesson 2 (25s):
- State the lesson
- Brief example
- Why it matters

Lesson 3 (25s):
- State the lesson
- Brief example
- Why it matters

CTA (10s): "What's your experience? Comment below."

Format 2: Industry Commentary (90-120 seconds)

Hook (5s): "The [industry] is changing. Here's what you need to know."

Context (30s):
- Current state
- Why it matters
- Who's affected

Analysis (50s):
- Your perspective
- Supporting evidence
- Implications

Action (25s):
- What to do about it
- Next steps
- Resources

CTA (10s): "Thoughts? Let's discuss in the comments."

Format 3: Quick Tip (30-60 seconds)

Hook (5s): "Quick tip for [target audience]"

Problem (10s):
- Common challenge
- Why it's frustrating

Solution (30s):
- Your approach
- How to implement
- Expected outcome

CTA (10s): "Try this and let me know how it goes."

Format 4: Behind-the-Scenes (60-120 seconds)

Hook (5s): "A day in the life of [your role]"

Morning (30s):
- Key activities
- Priorities
- Challenges

Afternoon (30s):
- Main work
- Meetings
- Decisions

Evening (30s):
- Wrap-up
- Learnings
- Preparation for tomorrow

CTA (10s): "What does your day look like?"

Format 5: Case Study (120-180 seconds)

Hook (5s): "How we [achieved result] in [timeframe]"

Challenge (30s):
- Initial situation
- Problem faced
- Stakes

Approach (60s):
- Strategy chosen
- Implementation
- Key decisions

Results (40s):
- Specific outcomes
- Metrics
- Lessons learned

CTA (10s): "Questions? Drop them below."

CTA (Last 5-10 seconds)

[TEXT]: [Clear call-to-action]

[YOU]: [Engagement prompt]

CTA Options:

  • "What's your take? Comment below."
  • "Follow for more [industry] insights."
  • "Share this with your network."
  • "DM me if you want to discuss this."
  • "Check the link in comments for more."

LinkedIn Caption Formula

Line 1: [Hook - first 140 characters are crucial]

Line 2-4: [Context and value - expand on video]

Line 5: [Key takeaway or insight]

Line 6: [Question or engagement prompt]

Line 7: [Hashtags - 3-5 relevant]

Example:

The biggest mistake I see [professionals] make with [topic] 👇

After [X years] in [industry], I've noticed a pattern. Most people focus on [wrong thing] when they should be focusing on [right thing].

Here's what actually works:
• [Point 1]
• [Point 2]
• [Point 3]

The result? [Specific outcome].

What's been your experience with this? I'd love to hear your perspective in the comments.

#[Industry] #[Topic] #[ProfessionalDevelopment]

Visual Guidelines

Professional Appearance

  • Dress: Business casual or professional
  • Background: Clean, uncluttered, branded
  • Lighting: Even, professional
  • Framing: Head and shoulders or full body
  • Eye contact: Look at camera

Text Overlays

  • Font: Professional, readable (Sans-serif)
  • Color: Brand colors or neutral
  • Size: Large enough for mobile
  • Position: Lower third or center
  • Animation: Subtle, professional

B-Roll

  • Relevant: Supports your message
  • Professional: High quality
  • Branded: Consistent with your brand
  • Purposeful: Adds value, not distraction

LinkedIn Video Checklist

Pre-Production

  • Topic relevant to professional audience
  • Value proposition clear
  • Script written and timed
  • Professional setting prepared
  • Appearance professional
  • Equipment tested

Production

  • Good lighting
  • Clear audio
  • Professional background
  • Eye contact with camera
  • Confident delivery
  • Multiple takes recorded

Post-Production

  • Captions added (crucial for LinkedIn)
  • Text overlays professional
  • Branding consistent
  • Audio clear
  • Length optimized (under 3 min)
  • Cover image selected

Publishing

  • Compelling caption written
  • First 140 characters optimized
  • Relevant hashtags added (3-5)
  • Tagged relevant people/companies
  • Posted at optimal time
  • Native upload (not link)

Best Posting Times

B2B Optimal Times

  • 7-8 AM: Morning commute
  • 12-1 PM: Lunch break
  • 5-6 PM: End of workday

Engagement Strategies

In Video

  • Address viewer directly
  • Share personal experiences
  • Be authentic and vulnerable
  • Provide actionable value
  • Ask for opinions

In Caption

  • Ask specific questions
  • Request experiences
  • Encourage debate
  • Prompt tags
  • Invite DMs

In Comments

  • Ask follow-up questions
  • Tag relevant connections
  • Share additional resources
  • Continue the conversation

LinkedIn Video Best Practices

Do's ✅

  • Add captions (80% watch without sound)
  • Keep it professional but personable
  • Provide genuine value
  • Be authentic
  • Share insights, not just promotion
  • Engage with comments
  • Post consistently
  • Use native video upload

Don'ts ❌

  • Don't be overly salesy
  • Don't use clickbait
  • Don't ignore comments
  • Don't post low-quality video
  • Don't forget captions
  • Don't be too casual
  • Don't post irrelevant content

Analytics to Track

Key Metrics

  • Views: Total reach
  • Engagement Rate: Likes, comments, shares
  • Click-Through Rate: Link clicks
  • Follower Growth: New connections
  • Profile Views: Interest level
  • Video Completion: Watch time
  • Shares: Viral potential

Success Indicators

  • Views: 3-5x your connection count
  • Engagement: 2-5% is good for LinkedIn
  • Comments: Quality over quantity
  • Shares: High value indicator
  • Profile Visits: Strong interest

Troubleshooting

Low Views

  • Post at better times
  • Use relevant hashtags
  • Tag relevant people
  • Engage with others' content

Low Engagement

  • Ask better questions
  • Make content more relatable
  • Provide more value
  • Reply to all comments
  • Create conversation starters

Low Shares

  • Make content more valuable
  • Create "save-worthy" content
  • Add clear takeaways

  • Provide unique insights
