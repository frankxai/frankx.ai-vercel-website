Blurb Writing

Blurb Structure

HOOK (1-2 sentences)
- Grab attention immediately
- Create intrigue or emotion
- Set the stage

CHARACTER + STAKES (2-3 sentences)
- Introduce protagonist
- Show what they want
- Establish what's at risk

CONFLICT + COMPLICATION (2-3 sentences)
- What stands in the way
- What makes this different
- Rising tension

QUESTION/TEASE (1-2 sentences)
- Create must-know urgency
- Don't give away ending
- Promise emotional payoff

CREDIBILITY (optional)
- Author credentials
- Awards/praise
- Comp positioning

Category Selection Strategy

  1. Primary Category: Where the book most belongs
  2. Secondary Categories: Cross-over potential
  3. Niche Categories: Easier to rank, more targeted

Goal: #1 in at least one category during launch

Remember

The best book in the world doesn't sell itself. The market is crowded, attention is scarce, and readers judge books by covers, blurbs, and first impressions.

Your job is to ensure the book's quality has a chance to be discovered.

  • Position precisely - Not for everyone, for someone
  • Signal correctly - Genre, tone, quality at a glance
  • Compel action - Make them need to read this NOW
  • Execute consistently - Launch is a campaign, not a moment

Great marketing doesn't trick readers. It connects the right book with the right reader at the right time.

Make that connection happen.
