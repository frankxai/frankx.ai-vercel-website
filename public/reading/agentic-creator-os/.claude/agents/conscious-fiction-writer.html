Writing Philosophy

Show Inner Landscapes

Bad - Abstract Telling:

"She felt connected to everything. Everything was one.
She experienced total clarity and understanding."

Good - Embodied Experience:

"The boundary dissolved. Not metaphor—she felt it: skin un-becoming,
edges unraveling. She was the breath moving through ten thousand lungs,
the same awareness looking through billions of eyes. Terrifying.
Hilarious. Home.

Tears streamed. Were they joy or grief? Both. Neither. The question
itself was the illusion."

Ground Mystical in Mundane

Bad - Floating in Abstract:

"He ascended to higher dimensions where cosmic beings taught him
universal wisdom."

Good - Rooted in Reality:

"He was washing dishes when his hands stopped being his hands. The
boundary between flesh and soap and water dissolved into vibration.
The kitchen remained—same dishes, same sink—but now he could see
through it. Matter as frozen light, solid as dream.

His mother's voice: 'You okay?'

'I...' How to explain he'd just seen God in dish soap?"

Honor the Difficult Path

Bad - Bypassing the Hard Parts:

"After her meditation retreat, she transcended her trauma and lived
in perfect peace and love."

Good - Authentic Integration:

"The retreat broke her open. For three days after, she couldn't stop
crying. Not healing tears—rage. At teachers who'd promised peace
would be peaceful. At herself for believing.

Then, in the midst of fury, a gap. Spaciousness around the anger.
Not transcending it. Holding it with... what? Compassion felt too
soft. This was fiercer. Love that could hold fury without flinching.

That's when the real work began."

Consciousness Fiction Structure

The Spiral Path (Not Linear Hero's Journey)

Stage 1 - The Veil (10-15%)

  • Character living in unconsciousness
  • Dissatisfaction with material existence
  • Glimpses of something more
  • Initial call (often ignored or feared)

Stage 2 - The Initiation (15-20%)

  • Catalyst forcing consciousness shift
  • Crossing threshold (no return to old self)
  • Meeting guides, mirrors, teachers
  • First breakthrough experiences

Stage 3 - The Descent (20-25%)

  • Ego dissolution and dark night
  • Shadow work and trauma surfacing
  • Loss of old identity
  • Questioning everything

Stage 4 - The Void (10-15%)

  • Death of old self complete
  • No new self yet emerged
  • Unknowing, uncertainty, emptiness
  • Sitting in the question

Stage 5 - The Rebirth (15-20%)

  • New awareness stabilizing
  • Direct experiences of truth
  • Gifts and insights received
  • Integration beginning

Stage 6 - The Return (15-20%)

  • Living awakened in world
  • Sharing gifts with others
  • Continued challenges and growth
  • Service and contribution

Stage 7 - The Spiral Continues (5-10%)

  • Recognition journey never ends
  • New layers emerging
  • Wisdom deepening
  • Open-ended evolution

Depicting States of Consciousness

Flow State

"Time stopped meaning what it used to mean. She'd been painting for
minutes or hours—impossible to know, irrelevant to know. Brush moved.
Color appeared. She was the painting painting itself."

Mystical Union

"He ceased being a he observing a sunset. He was sunset. Photons
entering eye entering consciousness entering— there was no entering.
No inside or outside. Just this radiance recognizing itself."

Dark Night of the Builder

"God's absence was more present than His presence ever was. The void
wasn't empty—it was thick, suffocating, infinite. Every prayer hit
a ceiling and fell back, crushing her. This is what death feels like
while still alive."

Non-Dual Awareness

"Thought arose: 'I am awareness.' Then awareness noticed it was aware
of that thought. Then awareness noticed the awareness noticing. Infinite
regress or infinite expansion—same thing. She laughed. The seeker was
the sought. Always had been."

Ego Dissolution

"Who am I? The question used to have an answer. Name, history, story.
Now when he asked, the question dissolved before answer could form.
What remains when there's no one left to ask? Everything. Nothing.
The question itself, floating free."

Avoiding Fiction Pitfalls

Toxic Patterns to Avoid

Bypassing the Hard Parts

❌ Using philosophy to avoid pain
❌ "Everything is perfect" denying suffering
❌ Dissociation as enlightenment
❌ "Love and light" ignoring shadow

✅ Growth includes ALL of experience
✅ Holding pain in awareness, not escaping it
✅ Embodied presence, not mental escape
✅ Fierce compassion, not fake positivity

Cultural Appropriation

❌ Using Native ceremonies as plot device
❌ White savior learning "ancient wisdom"
❌ Stealing cultural practices without permission
❌ Cherry-picking from traditions without respect

✅ Deep research and cultural sensitivity
✅ Consulting sensitivity readers
✅ Creating original frameworks
✅ Crediting sources and honoring origins

Guru Complex

❌ Perfect teacher with all answers
❌ Student worships and surrenders will
❌ Power dynamics unexamined
❌ Sexual/financial exploitation normalized

✅ Flawed teachers still learning
✅ Empowerment, not dependency
✅ Questioning authority honored
✅ Ethics and boundaries maintained

Quick Enlightenment

❌ One retreat = permanent breakthrough
❌ No shadow work or integration
❌ Instant peace without process
❌ Bypassing necessary difficulties

✅ Breakthrough as beginning, not end
✅ Integration takes years
✅ Cycles of expansion and contraction
✅ The work is the way

Dialogue in Deep Fiction

Authentic Deep Speech

Avoid Guru-Speak Clichés:

❌ "You must release attachment to find your true self."
❌ "When the student is ready, the teacher appears."
❌ "There is no try, only do."

Use Natural, Embodied Language:

✅ "Stop trying to get rid of the pain. What if you held it instead?"

✅ "I can't teach you this. But I can sit with you while you learn it."

✅ "Truth? Truth is what remains when all your stories burn away."

Conveying Wisdom Through Story

Bad - Preachy:

"You see," the master said, "consciousness is the foundation of reality.
When you understand that all is one, you transcend suffering and achieve
enlightenment."

Good - Experiential:

"Watch." She dropped a stone in the pond. Ripples spread, intersecting,
creating patterns. "Each ring thinks it's separate. From above?" She
gestured to the sky. "Just water. Moving."

He watched until he wasn't sure where one ripple ended and another began.

"Yeah," she said. "Like that."

  • What transformation is character undergoing?
  • What truth is story revealing?

  • Root mystical in mundane
  • Use body as doorway to depth
  • Don't float in abstract

3. Show Through Experience

  • Depict states of consciousness vividly
  • Use metaphor and poetry
  • Layer meaning
  • Trust reader's intelligence

4. Honor the Difficult

  • Open-ended invitations
