Tweet 2: The key insight:

Suno doesn't follow instructions. It captures vibes.

"Make the drums louder" → Won't work "Powerful driving drums" → Works

Describe the feeling, not the action.

Tweet 3: The formula that works:

Genre + Tempo + Instruments + Vocals + Mood + Special Elements

Weak: "rock song with guitar"

Strong: "indie rock, driving energy, clean electric guitar arpeggios, raspy tenor vocals, nostalgic triumphant"

Tweet 4: Genre specificity matters:

Don't say: "electronic" Say: "deep house" or "synthwave" or "ambient techno"

Don't say: "rock" Say: "indie rock" or "arena rock" or "post-punk"

Specific = better results.

Tweet 5: Tempo/Energy words:

Low: slow, gentle, peaceful, ambient, dreamy Medium: steady, groovy, flowing, moderate High: fast, driving, intense, explosive, frenetic

These set the pace more than BPM numbers.

Tweet 6: Instrument specificity:

Weak: "guitar and drums"

Strong: "clean electric guitar arpeggios, tight snare, deep 808 bass, atmospheric synth pads"

Tell it what kind of sound, not just what instrument.

Tweet 7: Vocal styles that work:

  • "ethereal female soprano"
  • "raspy male tenor"
  • "whispered intimate"
  • "belting powerful"
  • "layered harmonies"

"Female vocals" is too vague.

Tweet 8: Special elements for uniqueness:

  • "528Hz healing frequency"
  • "lo-fi vinyl crackle"
  • "orchestral swells"
  • "choir harmonies"
  • "nature sounds"

These make tracks distinctive.

Tweet 9 (CTA): Template I use for every track:

[genre], [tempo], [2-3 instruments], [vocal style], [2-3 mood words]
