The 7 Movements

MOVEMENT 1: RHYTHM (Energy)

"The beat that drives everything"

Your rhythm is your physical foundation - the pulse that keeps you moving.

Musical Elements:

  • Tempo (pace of life)
  • Beat (daily energy)
  • Groove (flow states)
  • Rest (recovery)

Key Questions:

  • Is your tempo sustainable?
  • Do you have enough beats per day?
  • Are you in the groove or forcing it?
  • When do you rest?

Composition Exercise: "What's the tempo of your ideal day? Write out the BPM of each hour."

MOVEMENT 2: MELODY (Mind)

"The tune that runs through your thoughts"

Your melody is your mental and emotional life - the music playing in your head.

Musical Elements:

  • Notes (individual thoughts)
  • Phrases (thought patterns)
  • Motifs (recurring themes)
  • Key signature (emotional baseline)

Key Questions:

  • What melody plays on repeat?
  • Is it in a major or minor key?
  • What motifs keep returning?
  • Can you change the key?

Composition Exercise: "If your dominant thought pattern were a melody, would you want to listen to it? Write a new one."

MOVEMENT 3: HARMONY (Soul)

"When all parts align in resonance"

Your harmony is when your values, actions, and purpose align - true consonance.

Musical Elements:

  • Chords (aligned elements)
  • Consonance (agreement)
  • Dissonance (conflict)
  • Resolution (alignment)

Key Questions:

  • What creates harmony in your life?
  • Where do you feel dissonance?
  • What needs to resolve?
  • What's your root chord (core value)?

Composition Exercise: "List your top 5 values as notes in a chord. Do they sound good together?"

MOVEMENT 4: COMPOSITION (Craft)

"The art you create and share"

Your composition is your creative output - the music you make for the world.

Musical Elements:

  • Arrangement (structure of work)
  • Instrumentation (tools and skills)
  • Production (quality and polish)
  • Performance (delivery)

Key Questions:

  • What are you composing?
  • Do you have the right instruments?
  • Is your production quality improving?
  • Are you performing or just rehearsing?

Composition Exercise: "Write the tracklist of your creative output this year. What's the album called?"

MOVEMENT 5: AMPLIFICATION (Capital)

"How far your music travels"

Your amplification is your reach and resources - turning up the volume on your impact.

Musical Elements:

  • Volume (reach)
  • Channels (distribution)
  • Production value (investment)
  • Royalties (returns)

Key Questions:

  • How loud is your signal?
  • What channels carry your music?
  • Are you investing in production?
  • Are you earning from your art?

Composition Exercise: "If your creative work were on a mixing board, which faders need to come up?"

MOVEMENT 6: ENSEMBLE (Circle)

"The players in your orchestra"

Your ensemble is your relationships - the musicians who play alongside you.

Musical Elements:

  • Orchestra (full team)
  • Section (close collaborators)
  • Duets (partnerships)
  • Solo (individual work)

Key Questions:

  • Who's in your orchestra?
  • Is each section strong?
  • Who do you duet with?
  • When do you solo?

Composition Exercise: "Cast your orchestra. Who plays what? Who's missing?"

MOVEMENT 7: MASTERWORK (Legacy)

"The symphony you leave behind"

Your masterwork is your complete opus - the full integration of all movements.

Musical Elements:

  • Opus (complete work)
  • Movements (integrated parts)
  • Theme (unified message)
  • Finale (conclusion)

Key Questions:

  • What's your magnum opus?
  • How do the movements connect?
  • What's the unifying theme?
  • What finale are you writing toward?

Composition Exercise: "Write the liner notes for your life's album. What does it say?"
