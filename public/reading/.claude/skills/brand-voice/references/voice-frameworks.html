How to Derive Voice Attributes from Samples

When a creator can provide writing samples, derive voice from observation rather than asking them to self-describe. Self-description produces generic ("authentic", "conversational") while analysis produces specific ("punchy 8-12 word sentences", "leads with a data point").

Analysis Method

Read 5-10 samples and answer:

Sentence structure

  • Average sentence length? (count words per sentence across 3 paragraphs)
  • Mix of short/long? (rhythm varies, or consistent?)
  • Fragment usage? (intentional, frequent, rare, never)
  • Question usage? (rhetorical, real questions to reader, none)

Opening patterns

  • How do pieces start? (statement, question, data point, scene-setting, personal story)
  • What's the hook strategy? (contradiction, surprising claim, specific result)
  • Does "I" appear in the first sentence? (strong indicator of style)

Vocabulary and formality

  • Technical jargon level? (high / medium / low)
  • Contractions? (frequent = casual, rare = formal)
  • Slang or internet language? (never, occasional, frequent)
  • Hedge words? ("maybe", "I think", "possibly" — present or avoided)

Structural patterns

  • How do they organize information? (linear argument, lists, narrative, Q&A)
  • Do they use headers in long-form? (analytical) or flow through sections? (narrative)
  • How do they close? (CTA, question back to reader, summary, forward-looking statement)

Perspective

  • First person ("I") vs second person ("you") vs third vs no person?
  • Does the creator position as peer, teacher, practitioner, or observer?
  • Do they share personal context/experience or stay abstract?

Synthesizing into Attributes

After analysis, name 3-5 attributes using verbs and specifics, not adjectives:

❌ Weak: "Conversational and authentic" ✅ Strong: "Writes like a peer debrief, not a lecture — uses 'you' 3x more than 'I'"

❌ Weak: "Direct and confident" ✅ Strong: "Opens with the conclusion, explains after — never buries the lead"

❌ Weak: "Educational" ✅ Strong: "Teaches through specific examples, never through abstraction alone"

Voice Drift Detection

Signals that content has drifted from the defined voice:

Drift signal What it usually means
Opener starts with "In today's world..." Generic, no voice loaded
Multiple exclamation points Enthusiasm override, lost precision
"Amazing", "incredible", "awesome" Fell back to generic superlatives
Passive voice throughout Hedging, not taking a stance
Every sentence starts with "I" Self-focused, not audience-focused
Paragraph-length sentences Lost the rhythm, revise for pace
Ends with "What do you think?" Lazy engagement, not on-brand for most creators
Spiritual/motivational language Guru tone creeping in
