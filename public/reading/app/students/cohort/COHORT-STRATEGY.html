AI Creator Accelerator -- Cohort Strategy

Version: 1.0 | Author: Frank Riemer (FrankX) | Date: 2026-03-23 Status: Ready to announce | Batch 1: 44 students from Oracle presentation

Executive Summary

The AI Creator Accelerator is a free, 4-week build sprint where students ship a real AI-powered project under mentorship from an enterprise AI Architect. It is not a course. There are no lectures to passively consume. Students build, ship, and document -- and in doing so, they become the founding community of FrankX's GenCreator movement.

This strategy is designed to be announced tomorrow (2026-03-24) with a simple application form and a clear value proposition for students. Every element also serves Frank's long-term business goals: content amplification, talent pipeline, consulting credibility, and community growth.

1. Why "AI Creator" and Not "AI Developer"

The word "Creator" is deliberate:

  • It aligns with the GenCreator framework already live at frankx.ai/gencreator
  • It includes portfolio builders, product launchers, AND content creators -- not just coders
  • It signals a mindset: you are not learning AI, you are creating with AI
  • It differentiates from every generic "AI bootcamp" or "prompt engineering course"
  • It positions Frank as a creator-first architect, not an academic instructor

2. Cohort Structure: 4 Weeks, 3 Tracks

Week 0: Pre-Work (Before Day 1)

Students complete before the first live session:

Pick a track (A, B, or C) Self-selection before Week 1 5 min

Week 1: Foundation (Everyone Together)

Theme: "Set Up Your AI Workshop"

Day Activity Format Duration
Mon Live Session: AI tools landscape + choosing your stack Zoom/Meet 60 min
Tue Async: Set up personal brand (portfolio site, LinkedIn, GitHub) Discord check-in Self-paced
Wed Office Hours: Project idea review + track confirmation Zoom/Meet 30 min
Thu Async: Define project scope (1-page brief using template) Discord submission Self-paced
Fri Showcase: 2-min lightning intros -- "I'm building X because Y" Zoom/Meet 30 min

Deliverable by end of Week 1: Project brief submitted, tools installed, brand foundations set.

Tools covered in Session 1:

AI Builders v0, Bolt, Lovable, Replit Agent Ship without deep coding

Week 2: Build Sprint Begins (Track-Specific)

Theme: "Get Your Hands Dirty"

Track A: Portfolio Builder (Job Seekers)

Day Activity
Mon Live: Architecture patterns for portfolio projects (Frank's enterprise lens)
Wed Office Hours: 1-on-1 architecture review (5 min per student, rotating)
Fri Showcase: Show your WIP, get peer feedback

Focus areas:

  • Building a project that demonstrates problem-solving, not just tutorial completion
  • Architecture decisions that show systems thinking
  • README quality that impresses hiring managers
  • LinkedIn profile optimization with AI-generated content

Track B: Product Launcher (Entrepreneurs)

Day Activity
Mon Live: MVP scoping -- ruthlessly cut to shippable core
Wed Office Hours: Technical blockers + pricing/hosting strategy
Fri Showcase: Demo your MVP progress

Focus areas:

  • Minimal viable product, not minimal viable prototype
  • Landing page that converts (v0 or Lovable)
  • Pricing strategy for AI-powered products
  • Launch checklist preparation

Track C: Creator and Community (Content Builders)

Day Activity
Mon Live: Content strategy with AI tools (writing, video, design)
Wed Office Hours: Content review + growth tactics
Fri Showcase: Share your best piece of content this week

Focus areas:

  • Building in public (documenting the AI journey)
  • LinkedIn/Twitter/YouTube strategy for tech content
  • Community building fundamentals
  • Cross-promotion mechanics with frankx.ai

Week 3: Refine and Polish

Theme: "Make It Shine"

Day Activity All Tracks
Mon Live: Quality bar session -- Frank reviews 3 projects live, shows what "great" looks like 60 min
Wed Office Hours: Final architecture/content review before freeze 30 min
Fri Showcase: Dress rehearsal for Demo Day (practice pitches) 45 min

Key activities this week:

  • Code/content freeze by Thursday
  • Practice 3-minute demo pitch
  • Peer review pairs assigned (every student reviews 2 others)
  • Final LinkedIn/portfolio updates

Week 4: Ship and Launch

Theme: "Go Live"

| Day | Activity | | --- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------- | | Mon | Live: Launch strategy session -- how to announce, where to post, how to get traction | 60 min | | Tue | Launch Day: Everyone ships/publishes simultaneously (coordinated social push) | | Wed | Buffer day for fixes | | Thu | Demo Day Prep: Final pitch rehearsal | | Fri | DEMO DAY: Each student presents (3 min + 2 min Q&A) -- recorded | 3 hours |

4. Application and Selection

Application Form (Google Form or Tally)

Questions:

  1. Name and email
  2. LinkedIn profile URL (required -- filters for serious people)
  3. Which track interests you? (A: Portfolio Builder / B: Product Launcher / C: Creator)
  4. What do you want to build? (2-3 sentences. "I don't know yet" is acceptable if they explain what excites them)
  5. Why this cohort? (1-2 sentences. Looking for motivation, not eloquence)
  6. Can you commit 5+ hours/week for 4 weeks? (Yes/No)
  7. Will you create at least 1 public piece of content about the experience? (Yes/No -- required Yes)
  8. How did you hear about this? (Oracle presentation / LinkedIn / Referral / Other)

Selection Criteria

Everyone from the Oracle presentation gets in (Batch 1 is the founding cohort). The application form exists to:

  1. Create a commitment moment (psychological investment)
  2. Filter out people who will not show up
  3. Collect data for track allocation
  4. Set the expectation that content creation is mandatory

For future batches, selection will be competitive.

Rejection Language (for future batches)

"We received X applications for Y spots. We prioritized applicants with clear project ideas and demonstrated commitment. You're on our waitlist for Batch N+1, and we'll notify you when applications open."

7. Content Requirements for Students

Each student must produce at least ONE of the following by Demo Day:

Video 2+ minutes showing their project and what they learned YouTube, TikTok, or LinkedIn
Twitter/X thread 5+ tweets documenting their journey Twitter/X

Suggested content hooks:

  • "Week 2 of the AI Creator Accelerator. Here's what I've learned about [tool/pattern]."
  • "I just shipped my first AI product. Here's the 4-week journey."
  • "What I learned about AI architecture from someone who builds it in enterprise environments."

Content amplification strategy:

  1. Create a shared hashtag: #AICreatorAccelerator (or shorter: #GenCreator)
  2. Frank reposts/comments on every student post (algorithm boost)
  3. Weekly "best content" spotlight in Discord
  4. Demo Day highlight reel compiled from student videos

10. Graduation and Alumni Program
