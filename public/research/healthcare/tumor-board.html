AI-Powered Tumor Board

Enhancing clinical decision-making through automated case synthesis, literature retrieval, and intelligent recommendation generation using OCI GenAI.

The Tumor Board Challenge

Multidisciplinary tumor boards are the cornerstone of pediatric oncology care. These weekly meetings bring together pathologists, oncologists, radiologists, surgeons, and other specialists to review complex cases and formulate treatment recommendations. However, the current process faces several challenges that impact efficiency and consistency.

Preparing for tumor board requires significant manual effort. Clinicians must compile patient histories, laboratory results, imaging findings, and pathology reports from multiple systems. They must also review relevant literature, identify similar historical cases, and synthesize all information into a coherent presentation. This preparation can consume several hours per case, limiting the number of cases that can be thoroughly discussed.

Additionally, the institutional knowledge accumulated over decades of treating pediatric leukemia often resides in the memories of senior clinicians rather than in accessible, searchable formats. When experts retire, this valuable experience can be lost to the organization.

AI-Assisted Case Preparation

The AI tumor board system addresses these challenges through three interconnected capabilities powered by OCI GenAI and Oracle Database 26ai:

Tumor Board AI Workflow

1
Automated Case Synthesis
OCI GenAI (Cohere Command A) aggregates data from multiple sources into a structured case summary, highlighting key findings and discrepancies.
2
Literature RAG
Vector search in Database 26ai retrieves relevant publications, clinical guidelines, and trial eligibility criteria based on patient characteristics.
3
Similar Case Retrieval
Semantic similarity search identifies historical cases with comparable presentations and outcomes, enabling evidence-based discussions.
4
Recommendation Draft
The system generates preliminary treatment recommendations based on guidelines and similar cases, for clinician review and modification.

Case Synthesis with OCI GenAI

When a new case is submitted for tumor board review, the system automatically retrieves all relevant data from connected sources: electronic health records, laboratory information systems, PACS for imaging, and the pathology database. Cohere Command A (or Gemini 2.5 Pro for complex cases) processes this information and generates a structured case summary that includes:

  • Patient demographics and relevant medical history
  • Timeline of symptom onset and diagnostic workup
  • Summary of morphology, immunophenotyping, and molecular findings
  • Key prognostic factors and risk stratification
  • Outstanding questions for tumor board discussion

Literature Retrieval with RAG

Staying current with the medical literature is increasingly challenging. The AI tumor board system implements a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline using Oracle Database 26ai Vector Search to maintain an up-to-date knowledge base of published clinical trials, treatment guidelines from NCCN, COG, and AML-BFM, recent case reports, and ongoing clinical trial eligibility criteria. When a case is prepared, the system automatically queries this knowledge base using the patient's specific characteristics (genetic mutations, immunophenotype, risk factors) and retrieves the most relevant publications.

Similar Case Matching

One of the most valuable features is the ability to identify similar historical cases. The system uses embeddings generated by Cohere Embed 4 to create semantic representations of each patient's case, stored in Database 26ai's vector store. When reviewing a new case, the system retrieves the most similar historical cases based on genetic profile similarity, clinical presentation, patient age, and treatment response patterns.

Rare Subtype Cases

Identify similar cases across the entire AML-BFM network, even if the treating institution has never seen the subtype before.

Treatment-Resistant Cases

Find what alternative approaches worked for comparable patients not responding to standard therapy.

Clinical Trial Matching

Automatically identify open trials for which the patient might be eligible based on disease characteristics.

Outcome Prediction

Provide survival statistics from similar historical cases to support informed consent discussions.

Human-AI Collaboration

The AI tumor board system is designed to augment, not replace, clinical judgment. All AI-generated content is clearly labeled and presented as suggestions for clinician review. The final treatment recommendations are always made by the multidisciplinary team during the tumor board meeting. Audit trails track which AI suggestions were accepted, modified, or rejected.
