Pillar 1: Diagnostic Optimization

AI-Powered Diagnostic Pipeline

Integrating morphology analysis, immunophenotyping, and molecular genetics through OCI Vision and GenAI services to accelerate pediatric leukemia diagnosis.

The Diagnostic Challenge

Accurate diagnosis of pediatric acute leukemia requires the integration of three complementary analytical approaches: morphological assessment of blood smears, immunophenotyping through flow cytometry, and molecular genetic analysis. Traditionally, these analyses are performed sequentially by different specialists, with results integrated manually during tumor board meetings. This process typically requires 3-5 days for a comprehensive diagnosis.

The AML-BFM Study Group has accumulated over 40 years of diagnostic data, including morphology assessments from more than 2,000 children, immunophenotyping records from 1,400+ cases (evolving from 3-color to 10-color panels), and molecular genetics data from 500+ patients analyzed with a 116-gene NGS panel. This dataset represents a unique opportunity to train AI models that can assist pathologists and accelerate the diagnostic process.

Stage 1: Morphology Analysis with OCI Vision

Blood smear analysis remains the first-line diagnostic tool for leukemia detection. Pathologists examine Giemsa-stained peripheral blood or bone marrow aspirates to identify blast cells, assess their morphological features, and classify them according to the FAB or WHO criteria. This process requires significant expertise and is subject to inter-observer variability.

OCI Vision Custom Models can be trained on the AML-BFM morphology dataset to assist pathologists in blast detection and classification. The training dataset includes labeled images with expert annotations identifying:

  • Blast cells vs. normal hematopoietic cells
  • Morphological subtypes (FAB M0-M7 for AML, L1-L3 for ALL)
  • Specific cytological features (Auer rods, nuclear shape, cytoplasmic granulation)

The custom vision model processes digitized blood smears and provides probability scores for each cell type, highlighting areas requiring pathologist attention. This approach aims to reduce review time while maintaining diagnostic accuracy through human-AI collaboration.

Stage 2: Immunophenotyping Analysis

Flow cytometry immunophenotyping has evolved significantly over the past four decades. The AML-BFM dataset spans this evolution, from early 3-color panels to current 10-color configurations that can simultaneously analyze multiple surface and intracellular markers. This heterogeneous data presents both a challenge and an opportunity for AI-assisted analysis.

OCI Data Science provides the computational environment for developing dimensionality reduction algorithms that can:

  • Normalize data across different instrument generations and panel configurations
  • Apply UMAP/t-SNE for population visualization
  • Automatically gate populations based on learned patterns
  • Detect minimal residual disease (MRD) at sensitivities below 0.01%

Stage 3: Molecular Genetics with GPU Acceleration

The 116-gene NGS panel used by AML-BFM covers the most clinically relevant mutations, fusions, and copy number variations in pediatric leukemia. Processing this data requires significant computational resources, particularly for variant calling and annotation.

NVIDIA Parabricks on OCI accelerates genomic analysis workflows from hours to minutes. The GPU-optimized pipeline includes:

  • BWA-MEM2 alignment (GPU-accelerated)
  • GATK HaplotypeCaller for variant calling
  • Custom annotation with ClinVar, COSMIC, and AML-BFM-specific databases
  • Cryptic fusion detection using RNA-seq data

A typical sample analysis that previously required 24 hours on CPU-based infrastructure can be completed in approximately 30 minutes using A10 or A100 GPUs on OCI, enabling same-day results for urgent cases.

Integration and Reporting

The final stage uses OCI GenAI (Cohere Command A or Gemini 2.5 Pro) to synthesize findings from all three analytical modalities into a structured diagnostic report. The LLM is prompted with the individual analysis results and generates a narrative summary that:

  • Correlates morphological findings with immunophenotype
  • Interprets genetic variants in clinical context
  • Suggests WHO 2022 classification
  • Highlights discrepancies requiring expert review
  • Provides references to relevant literature

All AI-generated content is flagged as such and presented to the reviewing pathologist for validation before becoming part of the official medical record.
