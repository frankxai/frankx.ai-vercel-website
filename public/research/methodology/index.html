How This Research Is Conducted

Transparency builds trust. This page documents the research process, source hierarchy, validation methods, and maturity model used across all research in this center. Every claim is traceable to a verifiable source.

Last updated: Feb 6, 2026 Research Process v2.0

1. Research Process

Every research article follows a five-stage process designed to maximize accuracy while maintaining practical relevance for enterprise architects. The process is iterative—findings from later stages can trigger re-investigation of earlier assumptions.

Stage 01
Landscape Scan
Systematic search across official documentation, academic papers, industry analyst reports, and GitHub repositories. Minimum 15 distinct sources per topic before synthesis begins.
Stage 02
Source Verification
Every factual claim is traced to an official or primary source. Pricing data is verified against vendor price lists. Feature claims are checked against release documentation. No second-hand claims accepted.
Stage 03
Pattern Synthesis
Cross-reference findings across sources to identify architectural patterns. Resolve contradictions between vendor claims and independent evaluations. Prioritize patterns validated by multiple independent sources.
Stage 04
Architecture Validation
Every architecture diagram is validated against real-world deployment constraints: cost, latency, compliance requirements, and operational complexity. Abstract patterns are tested against concrete implementation scenarios.
Stage 05
Publication & Review
Final review against accuracy checklist. Sources cited with direct links. Maturity indicator assigned. Regular review cycle ensures content stays current as the field evolves rapidly.

2. Source Hierarchy

Not all sources carry equal weight. This research uses a three-tier source hierarchy, with primary sources always taking precedence when conflicts arise.

Tier 1 — Primary Official Documentation & Primary Sources

Vendor documentation, official release notes, API specifications, peer-reviewed papers, and GitHub source code. These are authoritative and always preferred.

Examples: Oracle documentation (docs.oracle.com), Anthropic research papers, Cohere model cards, official GitHub repositories (oracle-quickstart, oracle-devrel), IEEE/ACM publications, EU legislative texts.

Tier 2 — Validated Analyst Reports & Expert Analysis

Research from established analysts (Gartner, McKinsey, BCG, Forrester), recognized industry experts, and reputable technology publications. Used to provide market context and strategic framing.

Examples: BCG AI Agent frameworks, Gartner Hype Cycles, McKinsey AI adoption surveys, InfoWorld technical reviews, The Register analysis.

Tier 3 — Supporting Community & Ecosystem Sources

Blog posts, conference talks, community benchmarks, and practitioner reports. Used for supplementary context but never as sole evidence for architectural claims. Cross-referenced against Tier 1/2 sources.

Examples: Engineering blog posts, conference presentations, Stack Overflow discussions, community benchmarks.

3. Validation Rules

Rule: No Unverified Pricing Claims

Pricing data is model-dependent and time-sensitive. Every cost claim states the specific models compared, cites the source URL, and includes the verification date. Blanket statements like "X is cheaper" without specifying which models are never used. Pricing is verified against official price lists, not cached knowledge.

Rule: Architecture Before Aesthetics

Every architecture diagram is validated for technical accuracy before visual polish. Pipeline stages must be correct (e.g., reranking is retrieval, not orchestration). Enterprise diagrams include three tiers: Security/Governance (top) + Pipeline (center) + Observability/Evaluation (bottom). No diagram is published without passing the architecture validation stage.

Rule: No Hallucinated Features

AI models can hallucinate product capabilities. Every feature claim about a specific product or service is verified against that product's official documentation. If a feature cannot be confirmed in official docs, it is not included, regardless of how plausible it sounds.

Rule: Honest Limitations

Research openly acknowledges limitations and gaps. Where data is insufficient, where comparisons are approximate, or where the author's experience may create bias, this is stated explicitly. The goal is accuracy, not advocacy.

Rule: Confidentiality by Design

All architecture patterns are anonymized. Patterns are derived from real-world enterprise engagements, but no proprietary customer data, identifiers, or organization-specific details are ever included. Research reflects general architectural patterns applicable across industries.

5. Author Context

This research center is maintained by an enterprise AI cloud architect working on real-world AI system deployments across industries including healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, and public sector. The research reflects patterns encountered in production engagements, validated against official documentation and industry best practices.

Areas of expertise: Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI), multi-agent orchestration, RAG architecture, LLM cost optimization, sovereign AI deployment, healthcare AI systems, database-native AI patterns.

Research tools used: Multi-agent research orchestration with AI-assisted source discovery, followed by human verification of all claims against primary documentation. Architecture diagrams are generated with AI assistance and validated for technical accuracy before publication.

6. Update Cadence

Enterprise AI evolves rapidly. This research center follows a structured update cadence to ensure content remains current and accurate.

  • Weekly: Source verification check on pricing data and model availability
  • Bi-weekly: New research findings incorporated into existing portals
  • Monthly: Maturity assessment review across all research areas
  • Quarterly: Full content audit against latest vendor documentation
  • Event-driven: Major product announcements trigger immediate review of affected research areas
Feedback & Corrections

If you find an error, outdated information, or have a suggestion for improvement, corrections are welcomed. Accuracy is prioritized over coverage—it is better to fix an error than to add new content.
