## CONCEPT

A cinematic still photograph from the doorway of a working studio at the precise moment before dawn. The room is dim, lit primarily by a single warm desk lamp and the cold blue-cyan glow of an open laptop screen on the desk — a Lenovo, lid half-tilted, a code editor visible but unreadable from this angle. Beside the laptop: a porcelain coffee cup, steam rising in a thin diagonal line caught by the cold screen-light. Behind the desk, a wide window. Outside the window: the night-to-day transition — black at the top, deep cobalt in the middle, the first grey-pink hint of approaching dawn at the bottom horizon. The room is quiet, sacred, mid-thought.

## SCENE

The view is from behind the chair, slightly elevated — the photographer is standing where someone has just stepped away. The chair is empty. Recently occupied. Warm wood floor. A single open notebook on the desk corner, pages turned over, ink visible but text unreadable. No human figure in frame.

## COMPOSITION

Rule-of-thirds. Laptop and lamp on the left third. Window on the right third. Empty chair centered low. The horizon line outside the window aligns with the upper third inside the room. Strong diagonal from the steam line to the window glow.

## LIGHTING

Two-source: warm tungsten desk lamp at 3000K soft on the left, cold cyan-blue laptop screen-light at 6500K on the right. The two lights do not mix harshly — the room reads as the meeting point of two intelligences. Outside light is the cold pre-dawn at 10000K. Subtle volumetric atmosphere catches the steam.

## PALETTE

Deep matte black walls #0a0a0b — the FrankX void.
Warm tungsten #f59e0b and #fcd34d — the soul lamp.
Cold tech cyan #06b6d4 and #0e7490 — the screen.
Pre-dawn cobalt fading to grey-pink — the horizon.
No saturation outside this palette. Cinematic, not overlit.

## MOOD

The hour before the day begins. Reverence. Concentration without performance. The studio of someone who has already started. Not lonely — alive. Not glamorous — true.

## STYLE REFERENCES

Studio Ghibli production stills, the still-life moments. Roger Deakins interior cinematography in Blade Runner 2049, the warm-cold lighting dialectic. The empty-room aesthetic of Vermeer, light through a window as the subject. AVOID: tech-bro hustle, neon glow, glitch effects, AI-generated faces, abstract gradients posing as art, generic studio stock.
