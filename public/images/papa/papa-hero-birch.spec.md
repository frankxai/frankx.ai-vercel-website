## Variant 1 — primary (use this)

A grove of slender white birches at first light, photographed in cinematic medium-format quality. The composition is vertical-tall trees against a dark dawn sky transitioning to soft amber at the horizon. Mist hangs at knee height between the trunks. The light filters through the leaves in selective shafts, picking out the bark in luminous whites and pale silvers against deep void-black background. No human figure. No fabricated face. No text overlay. The mood is reverent, slow, contemplative — the kind of light Andrei Tarkovsky filmed in Mirror (1975) or Andrei Rublev (1966). Inspiration: the deep amber-on-black palette of a Caravaggio chiaroscuro applied to a Russian birch landscape.

Birches carry meaning here — they are the Slavic symbol of memory, longevity, and the boundary between worlds. They mark the lineage from Volga German Kazakhstan back to northern Germany without needing a single word.

Camera: full-frame medium-format, 35mm equivalent lens, deep focus front to back, very low ISO for clean shadow detail. Hyper-real but never glossy. Hand-of-the-photographer present, never AI-perfect.

Color palette: void black `#0a0a0b` foundation, soul-amber accents `#f59e0b` `#d97706` in the dawn glow, pale birch white `#E8DDD0` for the trunks. No greens, no blues, no purples. Warm shadow temperature only.

Refusals: no people, no faces, no buildings, no roads, no signs, no animals, no text, no AI-perfect symmetry, no painterly impressionism, no oversaturated golden-hour cliché.
