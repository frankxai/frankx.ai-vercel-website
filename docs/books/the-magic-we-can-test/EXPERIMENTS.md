# The 28 Experiments

Each day includes a human opening, one law, one boundary statement, one experiment, one outward action, one AI prompt, one image prompt, and one evidence record.

## Movement I — The World Arrives

### 1. The First Astonishment

**Law:** Attention can turn the ordinary into information.  
**Experiment:** Observe one common object or scene for three uninterrupted minutes; record ten previously unnoticed details and five conditions required for its existence.  
**Human move:** Tell one person one specific thing you noticed and appreciate.  
**Evidence:** Did perception, mood, or behavior change?

### 2. The World You Notice

**Law:** Limited attention creates a selected experienced world.  
**Experiment:** Choose one cue—kindness, opportunity, beauty, or friction—and count its appearances for one day.  
**Boundary:** Attention changes detection and action; this does not prove that thought generated the external events.

### 3. The Gift Already Here

**Law:** Gratitude practices produce small average improvements in well-being, with individual variation.  
**Experiment:** Write ten concrete resources and the causal chain behind each; express one gratitude directly.  
**Human move:** Convert one appreciated resource into service or creation.

### 4. The Twenty-Watt Sun

**Law:** The brain performs extraordinary computation within a severe energy budget.  
**Experiment:** Track cognitive state across sleep, food, movement, stimulation, and focused work.  
**Boundary:** Personal “energy” is a bundle of subjective and physiological states, not a free-floating physical substance.

### 5. Breath: The Invisible Exchange

**Law:** Breathing links voluntary action with autonomic regulation.  
**Experiment:** Compare two minutes of normal breathing with two minutes of gentle, slower breathing; record state without forcing intensity.  
**Human move:** Pause for one breath before a consequential reply.

### 6. The Body as Instrument

**Law:** Interoception supplies information about internal bodily state.  
**Experiment:** Three times today, record sensation before interpretation: pressure, warmth, tension, pulse, hunger, fatigue.  
**Boundary:** A bodily signal is evidence, not a complete verdict about a person or decision.

### 7. The Time You Can Touch

**Law:** Clock time is measured uniformly; experienced time changes with attention, novelty, emotion, and memory.  
**Experiment:** Audit one day in 30-minute blocks and compare planned, lived, and remembered time.  
**Human move:** Reclaim one hour from low-value stimulation.

## Movement II — The Laws Under the Surface

### 8. Matter Keeps Agreements

**Law:** Stable structures emerge because matter behaves under consistent constraints.  
**Experiment:** Disassemble one ordinary object conceptually into material, forces, manufacturing, logistics, and maintenance.  
**Insight:** Reliability is accumulated agreement with constraints.

### 9. Energy Changes Form

**Law:** Physical energy is conserved while useful energy disperses.  
**Experiment:** Map the transformations behind one meal, commute, workout, server request, or generated image.  
**Boundary:** Do not equate thermodynamic energy with mood, virtue, aura, or money.

### 10. Chemistry Is Relationship

**Law:** Properties arise through interactions, concentration, temperature, structure, and catalysts.  
**Experiment:** Change one environmental variable around a recurring behavior and observe the result.  
**Metaphor:** A person or tool can be catalyst-like, but social causality is not a chemical reaction.

### 11. Entropy Sends an Invoice

**Law:** Ordered systems require maintenance and energy flow.  
**Experiment:** Find five neglected systems—body, home, code, relationship, finance—and pay one maintenance debt.  
**Human move:** Close or repair one small loop.

### 12. Feedback Makes Form

**Law:** Feedback loops stabilize, amplify, or destabilize systems.  
**Experiment:** Draw one personal loop: cue → action → result → signal → next action. Change one signal.  
**AI lens:** Ask the assistant to identify reinforcing and balancing loops without inventing hidden motives.

### 13. Probability Is Not Fate

**Law:** Uncertainty can be represented and updated; it is not the same as destiny.  
**Experiment:** Assign a probability to three beliefs, list evidence that would move each by 20 points, and update one after research.  
**Human move:** Replace one certainty statement with a calibrated estimate.

### 14. Measurement Is a Choice

**Law:** What a human system measures changes attention and incentives.  
**Experiment:** Replace one vanity metric with one outcome metric for seven days.  
**Boundary:** This is behavioral reactivity, not proof that human consciousness collapses quantum reality.

## Movement III — Imagination Becomes Instrument

### 15. The Predictive Mind

**Law:** Perception and action involve predictions corrected by incoming signals.  
**Experiment:** Before one event, write what you predict; afterward compare prediction with observation.  
**Boundary:** Predictive processing is influential, not a single settled theory of all cognition.

### 16. Memory Is Reconstruction

**Law:** Remembering reconstructs rather than replays a perfect recording.  
**Experiment:** Write one memory, then separate direct sensory detail, later interpretation, and uncertain inference.  
**Human move:** Preserve a family story with provenance rather than presenting memory as indisputable fact.

### 17. Imagination Is Simulation

**Law:** Mental simulation can prepare perception and action, but imagined success is not achieved success.  
**Experiment:** Visualize a difficult action including friction, then perform its first real step within ten minutes.  
**Evidence:** Did simulation reduce avoidance or merely produce reward without action?

### 18. Language Directs Attention

**Law:** Labels and questions foreground some distinctions and suppress others.  
**Experiment:** Rewrite one problem in three frames: threat, system, and opportunity; note which actions each frame reveals.  
**AI lens:** Have three assistants critique the framing, not merely answer it.

### 19. Identity Is Repeated Evidence

**Law:** Self-concepts are reinforced by memory, social feedback, and repeated behavior.  
**Experiment:** Choose one identity claim and create one piece of observable evidence for it today.  
**Example:** “I am a publishing founder” requires publication, not affirmation.

### 20. Environment Is Silent Instruction

**Law:** Defaults, cues, friction, and social context shape behavior.  
**Experiment:** Redesign one physical or digital environment so the desired action becomes easier and the unwanted action harder.  
**Human move:** Remove one object, app, or notification that repeatedly steals agency.

### 21. Relationship Multiplies Reality

**Law:** Attention, appreciation, expectation, reciprocity, and trust alter social behavior.  
**Experiment:** Give one precise appreciation and make one precise request. Observe the difference from vague positivity.  
**Boundary:** Gratitude does not remove incompatibility, consent, or another person's autonomy.

## Movement IV — Intelligence Beyond One Mind

### 22. The Machine That Answers Back

**Law:** Generative models predict and construct outputs from learned statistical structure and supplied context.  
**Experiment:** Give the same question to an AI with three different context packages; compare how the mirror changes.  
**Boundary:** Fluency is not truth, consciousness, wisdom, or intent.

### 23. Memory Outside the Skull

**Law:** External representations expand coordination and retrieval but introduce provenance, privacy, and drift problems.  
**Experiment:** Convert one book insight into a sourced note, personal application, image, experiment, and next review date.  
**Product bridge:** This is the Library OS chapter.

### 24. The Council of Agents

**Law:** Role separation can increase coverage and expose disagreement; orchestration also creates correlated error and complexity.  
**Experiment:** Ask four roles—scientist, skeptic, builder, humanist—to examine one decision, then make the decision yourself.  
**Boundary:** More agents do not equal more truth.

### 25. Judgment Is the Human Crown

**Law:** Optimization requires objectives; objectives encode values and exclusions.  
**Experiment:** For one AI-assisted decision, write the objective, constraints, irreversible risks, and who bears the consequence.  
**Human move:** Sign the decision with a named human owner.

### 26. The Quantum Is Not a Metaphor

**Law:** Superposition, interference, entanglement, measurement, and decoherence are formal physical structures with experimentally testable predictions.  
**Experiment:** Use an interactive quantum circuit simulator to observe interference; compare it with classical probability.  
**Boundary:** Quantum formalism does not imply thoughts attract macroscopic outcomes.

### 27. Reality Holds by Correction

**Law:** Quantum information is fragile; scalable computation requires detecting and correcting error faster than it destroys the logical state.  
**Experiment:** Design an error-correction loop for one human system: signal, threshold, correction, recovery, and learning.  
**Frontier lens:** Study Willow, verifiable quantum advantage, and the many-worlds interpretation while distinguishing result from interpretation.

### 28. Send the Magic Forward

**Law:** Knowledge compounds when it becomes transmissible practice and receives corrective feedback.  
**Experiment:** Select one future, define a 90-day hypothesis, three measures, weekly evidence, stop condition, and person served.  
**Transmission:** Publish one honest artifact containing result, limitation, and next experiment.

## Daily common protocol

Every day:

1. Record pre-state in under one minute.
2. Read the human opening and epistemic badge.
3. Run the experiment without changing the protocol midstream.
4. Complete the outward human move.
5. Use the AI companion optionally.
6. Save one visual or written artifact.
7. Record result, alternative explanation, and belief update.
8. Close with: **What changed because I paid attention?**
