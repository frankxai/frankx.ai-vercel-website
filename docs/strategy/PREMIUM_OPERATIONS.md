# FrankX Premium Operations

*The system for serving practitioners, executives, and ultra-HNW clients on the same brand without one tier hollowing out the other. Built 2026-06-22.*

The honest principle: **don't elevate the public funnel to billionaire-tier — you'd kill the practitioner conversion that pays the bills.** Stack a private door on top. Different audiences need different friction, different copy, different design language. One brand, five doors, calibrated per tier.

---

## The Five Doors

| # | Door | Audience | Surface | Friction | Copy register | Pricing visibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Public read** | anyone | blog, lab, AI center | 0 | technical authority | n/a |
| 2 | **Practitioner intake** | mid-market hires (€5k–€50k) | `/contact` form | low | technical authority | ranges public |
| 3 | **Executive concierge** | C-suite, enterprise (€50k–€500k) | `/engagements/strategic-advisor` (gated) | medium — pre-qualified | calibrated, restrained | "scoped per engagement" |
| 4 | **Private engagement** | family office, board, billionaire (€500k+) | `/partners/[slug]` (signed cookie, named) | zero — a human | discretion: a name, a sentence, a reference | none — never public |
| 5 | **Inner Circle** | repeat / known clients | Signal, direct WhatsApp | none — established | conversational | n/a |

### Why this layering works

- **Most visitors never see doors 3–5.** They convert through 1–2 normally. The premium tiers don't dilute the practitioner funnel because they're invisible to it.
- **Visitors who *belong* in 3–5 self-select.** A CFO at an €800M company doesn't fill out a 6-field form. They want a name and a calendar slot. The form is correct friction for someone hiring you for a €15k workshop and wrong friction for someone hiring you for a €250k engagement.
- **The brand reads premium *because* the upper tiers exist.** Knowing Frank operates as a fractional Chief AI Architect for 2–4 organizations is itself a signal — even for buyers who'll only ever pay for templates.

## The Copy Ladder

Same brand. Three registers. Pick by surface, not by mood.

### L1 — Technical authority (doors 1–2)

> "Build agentic systems with Frank."
> "Workshop, sprint, or template pack. Outcome-framed engagements."

Direct. Concrete. Outcome-led. Conversion-tuned. Numbers beat adjectives. ✅ shipping now.

### L2 — Calibrated restraint (door 3)

> "Frank operates as a fractional Chief AI Architect for 2–4 organizations at a time. Engagement begins with a written architecture brief."

Type-driven. Fewer words. State capacity (scarcity by competence, not gimmick). State the artifact, not the pitch. Avoid words like "transformation", "journey", "elevate" — they're tells. ⚙️ shipping in this branch.

### L3 — Discretion (doors 4–5)

A name. A sentence. A reference.

> *"Frank built the agentic architecture for [Named Family Office]. Introduction available on request via [Named Partner]."*

Often no public page at all. A unique URL emailed once. Expires in 7 days. No CTA, because the reader is already on the call calendar.

### Banned across all tiers

`transformation` · `unlock` · `journey` · `awakening` · `next-level` · `elevate` (as metaphor) · `intentional` · `soul` · `vibration` · exclamation marks · stock-photo dreaming. The technical-authority register from `VOICE_ROUTER.md` is the substrate; the L2/L3 shifts are additive *restraint*, not replaced vocabulary.

## The Design Ladder

| Tier | Visual language | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| L1 (doors 1–2) | Cyan/emerald gradients, glow cards, GlowCard primitives, ample imagery | current `/`, `/build`, `/contact`, `/agentic-builder-lab` |
| L2 (door 3) | One color (off-white on near-black), type-driven, generous whitespace, no gradients, Dieter Rams restraint | `/engagements/strategic-advisor` (this branch) |
| L3 (doors 4–5) | A single page that reads like a typewritten note from a Madison Avenue tailor. No nav. No footer. Maybe a watermark. | `/partners/[slug]` (bespoke per client) |

The premium signal at L2/L3 is *what's missing*, not what's added. Restraint reads as wealth in a digital register the same way it does in physical retail. If we add visual noise to signal "premium," we read mid-market.

## The Five Overdelivers

The mechanics that turn "good" into "people quietly tell other billionaires about Frank." Apply across all tiers; calibrate the artifact per tier.

| # | Mechanic | What it is | Where it applies |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **24-hour artifact** | Within 24h of *any* sign-up, deliver something useful they didn't ask for. Workshop → 15-min Loom critiquing their stack. Sprint → written architecture critique. Executive concierge → 1-page strategic brief. Private → a phone call. | All tiers, calibrated per intent |
| 2 | **Named human, immediately** | Every inquiry gets a reply *from Frank* within 24h. Even "got it — real reply Thursday." Concierge English; their native tongue if European. Never a system-only response on doors 3+. | Doors 2+ |
| 3 | **One-page brief before any contract** | Free. Written. Becomes the artifact they show their board. Defines scope, success criteria, deliverables, timeline, price — all on one page. | Doors 3+ |
| 4 | **Weekly executive read-out + daily Loom** | Replaces Slack chaos. Async-first. The executive reads at 06:30; the team watches at 09:00. | Doors 3+ |
| 5 | **Asymmetric reference ritual** | 2h/quarter writing LinkedIn recommendations, intros to investors, blurbs for past clients' products. Word-of-mouth multiplier. | All tiers |

### Mechanic #1 in detail — the 24-hour artifact

Per intent, a specific artifact is named in the auto-acknowledgement to the requester (the operator notification stays focused on the message itself — Frank knows which artifact to send from the intent label):

| Intent | 24-hour artifact |
|---|---|
| `workshop` | 15-minute Loom critiquing their current stack/workflow ahead of the workshop |
| `sprint` | Written 1-page architecture critique before the kickoff call |
| `partnership` | Written 1-page partnership memo with proposed structure |
| `press` | Concierge reply within 24h; sample answers/quotes if relevant |
| `advisory` | 30-minute "office hours" voice-memo on the question they raised |
| `general` | A real reply, named human |
| `executive` (new) | 1-page strategic brief on the problem they named; private call slot offered |

The auto-acknowledgement email now states which artifact is coming, by intent. That promise itself is half the wow.

## The Funnel — Process Upgrade

| Stage | Mid-market (today) | Premium (target) |
|---|---|---|
| First touch | Form → email | Form + named human reply ≤ 24h + the 24-hour artifact |
| Qualification | Frank reads message | Frank reads + writes a 3-sentence brief on what they actually need |
| Discovery | Intro call | **Discovery doc written first**, then 45-min refinement call |
| Proposal | Email back-and-forth | **One-page engagement letter** + Stripe link + kickoff calendar in one email |
| Kickoff | Sprint starts | Kickoff doc + access list + 24-hour first deliverable |
| Execution | Weekly Slack | Weekly written executive read-out + daily Loom + async-first |
| Close | Engagement ends | Close doc + 90-day check-in + LinkedIn reference offer |

The doc artifacts here (qualification brief, discovery doc, engagement letter, kickoff doc, weekly read-out, close doc) are the next ACOS sub-skills to build under `/ops`. Frank produces them today; the skill formalizes the templates and the cadence.

## Languages

Two meanings, both relevant.

**Voice register** (more important) — per the copy ladder above, calibrated per surface.

**Multi-lingual** — Madrid base = EN default, ES + DE natural surfaces. Existing ACOS `trilingual-localizer` agent handles surface translation. For doors 3+, language is more about *register* than locale: ultra-HNW European clients expect concierge English (or their native tongue from a real human, never machine-translated). Implementation: keep public surfaces EN-first, offer DE/ES on key landing pages when relevant, and never auto-translate door-3+ content.
