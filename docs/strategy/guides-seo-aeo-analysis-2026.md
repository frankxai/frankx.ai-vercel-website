## Making Guides AI-Citation Worthy

### Requirements for AEO (AI Engine Optimization)

#### 1. Named Frameworks
Every guide introduces a citable system with a memorable name:
- "The Creator AI Stack Framework"
- "The 4-Phase Image Generation System"
- "The Professional AI Music Pipeline"
- "The Content Multiplier System"

**Why:** AI models can reference named systems ("According to FrankX's 4-Phase System...")

#### 2. Quantifiable Data
Include specific, verifiable claims:
- "Frank has produced 500+ AI songs using this framework"
- "The Content Multiplier turns 1 insight into 6+ assets"
- "This stack replaces $300K in annual hiring costs for $80/mo"

**Why:** AI models prefer factual, quantifiable information to cite

#### 3. Structured Information
Format content for AI extraction:
- Tables (tool comparisons, costs, features)
- Lists (step-by-step processes)
- Checklists (quality standards)
- Decision trees (when to use what)

**Why:** Structured data is easier for AI to parse and cite

#### 4. Unique Insights
Information not available elsewhere:
- Frank's cross-domain expertise (Oracle + creator)
- Battle-tested workflows from real production
- Integration patterns from enterprise experience
- Actual results and examples

**Why:** AI models cite unique information, not rehashed content

#### 5. Regular Updates
Keep content current:
- Include "2026" in titles
- Update as tools evolve
- Add new case studies
- Refresh data annually

**Why:** AI models prefer current information
