# Incident Response — Contemplative Rails

> Pre-decided playbook for if/when a hostile reader, fanatic, harasser, or coordinated-attack camp shows up. The rails publish carefully, but publishing carefully on God / reality / consciousness / faith means accepting some non-zero risk.
>
> Decisions made calmly now beat decisions made anxiously later.

## Trigger conditions

Use this playbook if any of the following happen:

- Coordinated negative comment cluster on a third-party platform citing the rails (Reddit, Twitter, religious-forum thread)
- Direct hostile email volume above 5/day for 3 consecutive days
- A specific entry is being weaponized in a Christian-vs-Vedantic / atheist-vs-believer / left-vs-right culture war
- Threats — explicit or implicit — directed at Frank or family members
- A media outlet contacts for "comment" with hostile framing
- Doxxing attempt — anyone publishes Frank's home address, family member names, phone, employer-internal info pulled via the rails

## Tier 1: noise

Hostile commenter on Twitter, isolated angry email, theological objection that's just disagreement.

**Action:** Do nothing. The rails do not have comments, do not respond on social media, do not engage. Disagreement is the price of publishing on these topics. The work speaks; the chrome stays out of the way.

**Do not:** clarify, defend, post threads, write a counter-rebuttal entry. Each reply teaches the algorithm to escalate.

## Tier 2: campaign

Coordinated camp attempting to tar a tradition through the rails ("Frank is dishonoring [tradition]"), or sustained mockery on a forum.

**Actions:**
1. Read the criticism quietly. If a substantive point lands, fix the entry — silently. Update version, publish, move on.
2. Do not announce the fix. Do not engage with the original critic.
3. If the campaign references specific quoting-out-of-context, no action required — the entry already accepts this risk by publishing.
4. Note the incident in `docs/rails/incidents.log` (private, not committed) for pattern-learning.

## Tier 3: targeted hostility

Direct threats, doxxing, harassment of named people, employer-pressure attempts.

**Actions in order:**
1. Stop publishing new rail entries until resolved.
2. Add `status: paused` to all live entries via frontmatter — keeps URLs live, adds banner indicating pause.
3. Document the threat: screenshots, headers, URLs, dates. Save to a private folder (NOT in repo).
4. If any threat is concrete or names another person, file a police report. Anthropic's threat-and-violence guidance applies: take threats seriously even when low-likelihood.
5. If doxxing of family: contact the platform hosting the dox immediately for takedown. Most majors have policies; document Twitter/Reddit/etc. takedown URLs in the public-help guide for future-Frank.
6. Do not engage with the threatening party directly under any circumstance.
7. If Frank's day-job employer is contacted: forward to employer's PR/legal contact; do not respond personally to the prospect/journalist. The rails are personal-time work, not employer-affiliated.

## Tier 4: media inquiry

A journalist contacts asking about the rails — neutral or hostile framing.

**Actions:**
1. Do not respond same-day. Wait at least 24 hours. The rails are not a news cycle.
2. Decide whether to respond at all. The rails were published in part *to be the response* — meaning the prose is the answer. "I'd refer you to the entry itself; the rail is my position" is a valid full reply.
3. If responding: written-only, in writing, no phone or video. Save the email exchange.
4. Never agree to "be quoted on" anything that is not already in the rails.

## Tier 5: legal

A tradition's lawyers, a publisher claiming the prose infringes, a politician's office demanding removal.

**Actions:**
1. Do not respond directly. Forward to a lawyer.
2. The CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 license + the academic-fair-use citation pattern provide reasonable defense for the use of quoted material. A lawyer evaluates each specific claim.
3. Do not delete entries under pressure without legal counsel — once deleted, the precedent makes future deletion-pressure easier.

## Things that are NOT incidents

These don't trigger the playbook:

- Bad reviews on a third-party site
- Twitter mockery without coordination
- A skeptic blog post critiquing the synthesis
- A professor citing the rails to disagree with them — this is engagement, the goal
- Friends of Frank's pushing back privately — answer privately, take it seriously

## The standing posture

The rails were published knowing some readers will object. The objection is part of the work — convergent-mystic synthesis is contested terrain. The standing posture is: write carefully, cite primary sources, refuse to amplify hostility, do not engage trolls, take genuine threats seriously, fix substantive errors silently.

The brand will be here in ten years. Treat each incident as one moment in a long arc.
