# Hardware Intelligence Hub — product specification

## Product role

- Surface: hybrid editorial product.
- Business role: authority, lead qualification, affiliate conversion, and activation into FrankX learning products.
- Canonical route: `/ai-hardware`.
- Machine-readable route: `/ai-hardware.json`.

## ICP

Primary users are technically literate founders, creators, developers, and small AI teams preparing to spend €2,000–€15,000 on a workstation or local inference node. They understand GPU, VRAM, and local models at a headline level but lack a defensible way to balance creative compute, memory capacity, uptime, cloud spend, power, and upgrade paths.

Their dominant anxieties are buying the wrong memory architecture, paying for benchmark prestige rather than useful throughput, and discovering that devices cannot combine memory after purchase. They require dated sources, explicit caveats, workload fit, and a phased plan.

## Session job and first win

Job: turn workload, cash, privacy, and growth constraints into a shortlist and fleet sequence.

First win: within two minutes, the visitor receives a primary setup, a lower-capital alternative, an expansion trigger, and a clear statement of what remains better in the cloud.

## Product loop

1. Choose budget and dominant workload.
2. Set privacy, uptime, and team constraints.
3. Inspect the recommended compute mix.
4. Compare the recommendation against alternatives.
5. Review model and media fit, caveats, and evidence.
6. Save/share the URL or continue into the learning and architecture hubs.

## Proof and boundaries

- Every time-sensitive claim carries an observed or reviewed date.
- Official specifications and vendor claims are labelled separately from editorial inference.
- Retail prices are planning bands or dated observations, never presented as guaranteed live prices.
- Affiliate links must be labelled; commercial relationships never change ranking logic.
- Memory across heterogeneous devices is not described as one shared pool.
- Trillion-parameter training is explicitly treated as data-centre work.

## Information architecture

1. Decision hero and live compute constellation.
2. Setup planner.
3. Recommended system archetypes.
4. Hardware class comparison.
5. Model and creative-workload fit.
6. Expansion and fleet architecture.
7. Regional ecosystem and emerging accelerators.
8. Evidence, methodology, and FAQs.
9. Learning, research, and architecture continuation routes.

## Language brief

- Use: memory pool, VRAM, bandwidth, sustained load, inference node, workload routing, power envelope, fixed bill of materials, expansion trigger.
- Avoid: supercomputer hype, future-proof, no-compromise, ultimate, revolutionary, magic box.
- Emotional shift: from hardware anxiety to an inspectable capital plan.
- Primary CTA: `Plan my setup`.

## Release scope

V1 is a useful public planner and evidence-backed hub. Product-detail price ingestion, saved accounts, tracked affiliate attribution, benchmarks collected on FrankX-owned hardware, and plugin-generated exports are follow-on increments.
