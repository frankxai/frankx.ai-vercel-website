# Starlight Retreats — page specification

## Outcome contract

- Audience: creators, founders, educators, coaches, consultants and practitioners with meaningful work but no calm system for carrying it into ordinary life.
- Surface: public FrankX vision route at `/retreats`, plus one homepage teaser.
- First read: Starlight Retreats is a developing series of small, human-led working rooms and destination retreats—not a booked resort or passive wellness escape.
- First win: a visitor chooses the kind of progress they need and receives an honest recommended first room, its status and next action.
- Business role: qualify founding interest, route early demand to the prepared Purpose to Practice session, and establish the product ladder without pretending future formats are available.
- Proof: the prepared Tallinn working-session route, named participant artifacts and Retreat OS as an existing open operating foundation.
- Boundaries: no venue, date, price, availability, partnership, health outcome or Ana role is claimed. The generated destination image is labeled as representative.

## Product loop

Visitor intent → pathfinder recommendation → inspectable format/status → prepared proof route or founding conversation → later measured pilot evidence.

## Information architecture

1. Hero: representative destination image, literal offer, truth strip and two CTAs.
2. Why the room exists: insight → artifact → 30-day practice.
3. Pathfinder: four intent inputs mapped to the honest ladder.
4. Five-stage ladder: Prepared, Concept, In development, Designed method, Future.
5. Purpose to Practice proof: 90 minutes, 8–12 adults, two-page plan and dated 30-day trial.
6. Destination direction: mainland Spain first; Canaries/Madeira later; nothing confirmed.
7. Retreat OS and human authority boundary.
8. Founding conversation CTA.

## Release boundaries

- The route can be indexed when its claims audit and visual gate pass.
- No form or API is added. The primary conversion uses the prepared Purpose to Practice route.
- The stacked branch must not merge before PR #275, its base, is integrated or the branch is rebased safely.
