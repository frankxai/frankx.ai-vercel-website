Date: 2026-07-14  
Canonical route: `/experiences/tallinn-2026`  
Public aliases: `/experiences/mvu-tallinn-2026`, `/experiences/mindvalley-university-tallinn-2026`

## Outcome contract

- Audience: speakers, coaches, community and tribe leads, venue hosts, and attendees gathering in Tallinn around Mindvalley U 2026.
- Session job: understand how Frank can help a live session create a useful artifact and a stronger afterlife, then register a specific interest.
- First read: this is an independent Tallinn session and tribe studio, not an official Mindvalley program.
- First win: choose a role and desired outcome; receive an inspectable before / in-room / after amplification plan immediately.
- Business role: qualified workshop, speaking, collaboration, and venue interest through the existing consent-based FrankX intake pipeline.
- Boundary: no names or decisions about private collaborators; no hotel, budget, internal demand gate, private schedule, participant data, or implied event affiliation.

## ICP language

Use:

- room, session, audience, tribe, workshop, artifact, follow-through, host, speaker, venue
- shape, capture with consent, synthesize, package, continue, invite

Avoid:

- internal operating terms, agent swarm language, lead-gen theater, unconfirmed logistics
- therapy, certification, health, psychology, or guaranteed outcome claims
- generic “transformation” copy copied from Mindvalley
- all-caps labels and decorative AI language

## Creative direction

Thesis: **Make the room travel farther.**

Visual idea: one luminous signal moves through three states—before the room, inside the room, and after the room. The signal represents a speaker’s idea becoming a participant-owned artifact and a continuation path.

Material system:

- FrankX obsidian, graphite, emerald, cyan, and one restrained amber bridge accent
- Poppins display, Inter body, JetBrains Mono only for compact system labels
- existing `GlowCard`, `GlowButton`, and cursor-light behavior, used selectively
- official Mindvalley imagery framed as source-linked event context, never as FrankX-owned proof
- large editorial type, quiet negative space, hairline structure, rounded liquid surfaces

## Scene sequence

### Scene 1 — The room and its afterlife

Role: hook and qualification.

- Headline: “Make the room travel farther.”
- Deck: Frank helps speakers, coaches, tribe hosts, venues, and attendee-led rooms turn a live session into something people can use after they leave.
- Primary action: “Build my amplification plan.”
- Secondary route: official Mindvalley U event information.
- Visual: official 2026 Tallinn share image inside a clearly attributed external preview; FrankX signal path overlays remain outside the official image.
- Mobile: image becomes a 16:10 framed preview below the headline; CTA stays above the fold.

### Scene 2 — Session Amplifier

Role: interactive product loop and first win.

Input:

- visitor role: speaker, coach, tribe host, venue, attendee
- desired outcome: sharper room, participant artifact, useful follow-through, or a new workshop

Transformation:

- deterministic recommendation produces a three-stage amplification plan
- the selected plan changes the artifact, capture boundary, and next action

Inspectable result:

- before: framing and readiness artifact
- in-room: one human-centered participation or capture mechanic
- after: participant-owned resource or continuation path

Refinement:

- role and outcome can be changed without page navigation
- selected values prefill the interest form context

Reduced motion:

- all stages remain visible; no spatial travel or auto-advance
- cursor light and lift effects are removed

### Scene 3 — Four ways to collaborate

Role: show breadth without a generic feature grid.

1. Amplify an existing talk or workshop.
2. Co-design a small tribe session.
3. Host a useful room or offer a venue.
4. Join an attendee-led working session.

Use an asymmetric editorial rail, not equal floating cards.

### Scene 4 — Frank’s workshop studio

Role: honest proof and offer maturity.

- Proven and delivered: Ikigai & Branding, linked to the live workshop.
- Studio formats: AI agent building, AI music creation, and speaker-to-system sessions.
- Every studio format must be labeled as adaptable / proposed unless Frank has delivered it live.
- Link to `/workshops`, whose index must distinguish delivered work from studio drafts.

### Scene 5 — Official context, independent layer

Role: factual event grounding and trust.

- Official facts only: Tallinn, 20 July–2 August 2026, sourced from Mindvalley.
- Official 2024 Tallinn group image may appear as historical community context with the year in the caption.
- Explicit independent-event notice near imagery and in the footer.
- No copied proprietary font files. Learn from Mindvalley’s large grotesk scale; render with FrankX Poppins/Inter.

### Scene 6 — Register interest

Role: conversion without fake booking claims.

- Input: intent, role, name, email, project or organization, short context, consent.
- Submit to the existing `/api/intake` pipeline with `intent: workshop`.
- Success: acknowledge receipt and a real reply window.
- No payment, ticket, venue promise, schedule promise, newsletter enrollment, or bundled marketing consent.

## Motion score

- Runtime: React state + CSS transitions; existing cursor-light primitive where appropriate.
- Named job: reveal how one choice changes the session’s before / in-room / after plan.
- Signature move: the signal rail changes emphasis when the recommendation changes.
- Stillness: headings and body copy never animate.
- Track B: rejected. A pinned or scrubbed scene would slow the first win and add no product understanding.
- 3D: rejected. The interactive planning console is the more useful signature set-piece.
